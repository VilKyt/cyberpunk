# SCENE 2 — MISTY'S READING
*Act 2, Scene 2*

---

## GM Background

> *For the GM only.*

Misty Olszewski runs an esoteric shop in Watson — crystals, incense, tarot, the kind of place Night City tolerates the way it tolerates anything that doesn't cost it money. She is not a gimmick. She is genuinely perceptive, and her readings land in ways she cannot entirely explain, which she has accepted without drama. She does not perform mysticism. She receives it and passes it on.

Jackie Welles was her partner. He died recently — the Konpeki Plaza job, the heist, the way these things go in Night City. She is still in the part of grief where you work because stopping would be worse. The shop is orderly. She is orderly. The cards are what they have always been.

She has another client — V — who she reads for regularly. V is currently somewhere in the city, running out of time in a way Misty understands better than she lets on. She has not told V what the cards said last time. Some things you don't say aloud.

**The party is going to be the strangest reading she has ever done.** The cards respond to them with unusual clarity — like a signal with no interference. She will be unsettled in a quiet, professional way. She will complete the reading. She will not complete it entirely.

---

## Getting There

The shop is below the main street noise of Watson — incense and low light, the specific silence of a room where people come when things have gone wrong. Misty is behind the counter when they arrive. She looks at them for a moment.

She offers to read for them. She doesn't explain why. She just does.

She reads by question — she lays cards to answer what she asks of the deck, not in fixed positions. Watch the players' faces when she names each question. They don't know yet how accurate she is.

---

## The Reading

### QUESTION 1 — "Into what kind of city have they arrived?"

She lays two cards.

---

**THE FOOL** *(upright)*

> *"You entered this city without understanding its rules. You are inexperienced here, unprotected — walking near danger while believing you are still free. The city will test your innocence."*

She is not being cruel. She is describing exactly what she sees.

Core theme: naivety in an unfamiliar system. The players are powerful and have no idea how this city works, what its rules are, who sees them, how exposed they already are. That is The Fool.

---

**SEVEN OF CUPS** *(upright)*

> *"This city is built on illusion. Too many choices, too many false faces, too many promises. Nothing here shows its true form immediately."*

Core theme: hallucination, manipulation, temptation, artificial desire. Night City overwhelms outsiders with sensation and option — and beneath all of it, the systems that are actually running.

**Together:** a city that consumes outsiders through confusion, manufactured desire, and hidden machinery they do not yet understand.

> *GM note: The Fool is the party. The Seven of Cups is the city. Together they say: you walked into something that is designed to disorient people like you.*

---

### QUESTION 2 — "Is there a threat in the city? What kind?"

She lays three cards.

---

**SEVEN OF SWORDS** *(upright)*

> *"The danger does not attack openly. It hides, steals, manipulates — moves through shadows and institutions. By the time you feel it, it has already been here."*

Core theme: covert predation, corruption, extraction that looks like procedure. This is not an enemy that announces itself. It is a system.

---

**JUDGEMENT** *(upright)*

> *"Something dead is rising again, but incorrectly. People are returning — hollow. Altered. Called back by something that should have stayed buried."*

She looks at the card for a moment longer than the others.

> *"The city is full of people who are technically present. Still breathing. Still moving. And yet—"*

She doesn't finish. She moves to the third card.

Core theme: corrupted resurrection. People who are alive in a technical sense but psychologically, spiritually empty — consumed, functioning like echoes of themselves. The soul-drain, described without knowing what it is.

> *GM note: Judgement here is NOT the "find the dead man" card — that comes later. Here it describes what the Soulkiller network is doing to the population. Any magic-sensitive player will feel this card like a physical recognition.*

---

**FIVE OF PENTACLES** *(upright)*

> *"The city is becoming sick. Bodies weaken. Minds decay. People starve — spiritually, physically — and continue to wander through the streets unnoticed. This has been happening for a long time."*

She sets this card down carefully.

> *"The city itself is infected."*

Core theme: chronic suffering, slow depletion, sickness that has become background noise. Not sudden catastrophe — prolonged wasting. The soul-drain as a civic plague, operating for decades, normalised beyond recognition.

**Together:** covert extraction → people returning hollow → the entire population slowly emptying out. The threat is not an event. It is a condition.

---

### QUESTION 3 — "Who stands behind this threat?"

She lays two cards.

---

**THE DEVIL** *(upright)*

> *"The force behind this city feeds through bondage. Addiction. Greed. Control. Dependency. People believe they are choosing freely while being consumed."*

She doesn't name Arasaka. She doesn't name anything. She describes the architecture.

Core theme: enslavement through desire and systems. The chains in this card are loose — the figures could remove them. They don't. That is the horror of it.

---

**DEATH** *(upright)*

> *"This is not merely a man."*

She is quiet for a moment.

> *"It is a process of erasure. Identity, soul, memory, humanity — stripped away. Not killed. Unmade."*

Core theme: irreversible transformation, annihilation of self, death as machinery. Death upright is not an ending — it is a mechanism. Something that converts one thing into another, permanently.

**Together:** The thing behind the corruption is less a person than a living mechanism of consumption. A predator fused with the corporate machine, feeding on human essence while hiding behind power structures. The party may know this creature by another name. Misty only sees what it is, not what it calls itself.

> *GM note: do not name Arasaka. Do not name the Director. Misty has read something correctly and without a framework. "The Leech" is a useful internal shorthand — a predator ancient enough that the city has grown around it like flesh around a hook. Let the players place it.*

---

### QUESTION 4 — "What must they do?"

She lays three cards.

---

**THE HERMIT** *(upright)*

> *"You must seek someone hidden. A person who has withdrawn from society — not from weakness. Because staying visible would have killed them. They carry knowledge that the city cannot be allowed to know they have."*

Core theme: the hidden guide, exile by necessity, truth that survives only outside the system.

---

**NINE OF WANDS** *(upright)*

> *"The one you seek survived something that should have killed them. Wounded. Exhausted. Still standing — but only barely, and only through will."*

Core theme: a survivor of impossible trauma, enduring past the point they should have stopped. Not triumphant. Still fighting.

---

**KNIGHT OF SWORDS** *(upright)*

> *"There is another consciousness bound to them. Aggressive. Rebellious. Impossible to silence. It moves faster than the body containing it. It does not ask permission."*

She looks at this card with something that might be recognition.

> *"It is not them. It is inside them. And it has been fighting this fight longer than they have."*

Core theme: an invasive mind, a radical and relentless force occupying someone else's frame. A consciousness that refuses to stop.

**Together:** find the wounded survivor with the fractured mind. He carries both the key and the danger.

> *GM note: The Hermit is V — hidden, operating outside the normal city systems, carrying knowledge (the chip) that Arasaka would kill to recover. The Nine of Wands is V's condition — dying, running on borrowed time, still moving. The Knight of Swords is Silverhand's engram — the aggressive second voice, the consciousness that intrudes, the one who has been fighting Arasaka since before V existed. The party does not know any of this yet. Let them find it.*

---

*Misty's phone buzzes against the counter.*

She glances at it. Stands.

> *"One moment."*

She goes through the curtain at the back of the shop.

The party hears, faintly: one voice — tired, flat, the specific exhaustion of someone running on insufficient sleep. Then briefly, a second register. Rougher. More angular. Not a different caller. The same call, but a different voice within it — or behind it, or beside it.

Both voices stop. Misty comes back. She sits down.

She doesn't explain. She picks up the next card.

> *GM note: a magic-sensitive player who pays attention knows two things — there were two voices on that call, and one of them was not entirely present in the normal sense. Don't confirm or explain this. It sits.*

---

### QUESTION 5 — Personal: "Did someone recently die because of this?"

She holds the deck. She asks the question quietly, more to herself than to the room.

She turns one card.

---

**TEN OF SWORDS** *(upright)*

The image: a figure face-down, ten swords in the back. At the horizon, the sky is beginning to lighten.

Misty goes still.

> *"Yes."*

She doesn't say the name. She looks at the card for a long moment — specifically at the thin line of dawn at the edge of the image.

> *"This card came up in a reading I did recently. For someone else."*

She doesn't say whose.

> *"People think this is the worst card in the deck. It isn't. It means the worst thing has already happened — and the sun is still coming up."*

A beat.

> *"The death was not meaningless. Something changed because of it. I don't know if that's comforting. I've been trying to decide."*

She does not say Jackie. She will not say Jackie. But the card costs her something, and it shows.

Core theme: catastrophic ending, total betrayal by fate — and a dawn that rises anyway. The death was real. It mattered. It changed the shape of what comes next.

> *GM note: this is Jackie. The party doesn't know that. What they see is Misty containing grief with precision. That tells them more than the explanation would.*

---

## The Hidden Card

She begins to gather the spread.

Her hand pauses.

She has drawn one card more than she intended — or perhaps the deck pushed it forward. She looks at it. Her expression does not change, but something behind it does.

She turns it face-down on the table without showing it. Slides it to the side. Continues gathering.

If anyone asks what it was:

> *"Nothing you need to see today."*

She means it. She will not be pressed on this.

---

**THE TOWER** *(for the GM only)*

> *"The city will fall. Not because it was attacked from outside — because its foundations are already rotten. The corruption has grown too deep to remove. What is coming cannot be stopped. Only witnessed."*

This card does not mean: *you may fail.*

It means: *you arrived too late.*

The city is already spiritually dying. The Devil and Death revealed what is consuming it. The Tower reveals the final outcome: eventually the entire structure collapses under the weight of that corruption. The city is already past the point where healing is possible.

**That is why Misty hides it.** She is not hiding danger. She is hiding certainty.

She has read for enough people in this city to know: some truths destroy the will to act. These people in front of her — whatever they are, wherever they came from — are the first thing the cards have shown her that might matter. She will not take that from them before they've begun.

The Tower in this reading is not a challenge to overcome. It is the moment the inevitable finally becomes visible.

And that is very cyberpunk. The tragedy in Night City is never that evil appears. It is that the system was doomed long before the protagonists arrived.

> *GM note — what the Tower actually means:*
> *The collapse is not metaphor. It is the Soulkiller network reaching full deployment — the Director flipping the switch on a continental-scale soul-harvesting event. Every person in Night City who has ever used an Arasaka neural interface product, every citizen touched by the ambient drain, every Secure Your Soul subscriber — harvested simultaneously. Mass death or mass enslavement depending on whether the extraction holds at scale. Night City is the test case. If it works here, it scales globally.*
>
> *The city cannot be saved from this in any conventional sense. The infrastructure is already in place. The drain has been running for decades. The question the party is actually fighting over is not "will the Tower fall" but "what exists on the other side of it." Whether the Director wins completely — or whether something survives, escapes, or finds a way through — is the real shape of the campaign.*
>
> *Whether the Tower ever surfaces to the players is your decision. If Misty eventually tells them — if trust reaches that point — it reframes everything. The fight changes shape. Work it out in play.*

---

## After the Reading

She gathers the deck. She is quiet for a moment.

**On the city:**
> *"Don't let it thin you out. People have been thinned out by this city for decades and they don't notice while it's happening."*

**On the threat:**
> *"I see it sometimes in readings for people who work near the towers. Something in the background of everything. I always thought it was interference."* Pause. *"I don't think that anymore."*

**On finding the wounded survivor:**
> *"I don't know who it is. The cards gave me a shape, not a name. Someone fractured. Someone still standing. Someone carrying something that isn't entirely theirs."* She looks at the Knight of Swords still on the table. *"Find whoever that is. Carefully."*

**On the Ten of Swords:**
> *"I hope the worst has already happened to you."* She means it. *"I genuinely do."*

**On the hidden card:**
> *"There's nothing else."*

She will not be moved from this.
