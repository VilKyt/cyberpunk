# SCENE 1B — THE WATSON ALLEY
*Act 2, between Scene 1 and Scene 2*

---

## GM Background

> *For the GM only.*

Arasaka's Threat Anticipation division has had the tattoo parlor under passive surveillance for roughly eight months. They don't know what the ink does — their equipment registers the Artist's clients as soul-clear, which should be impossible without proprietary Arasaka shielding. The anomaly is logged. Periodically reviewed. It has not yet been escalated because there is no pattern of *behaviour* to escalate — just a small shop whose clientele reads wrong on calibration scans.

Six weeks ago, a low-level TA analyst flagged it for active monitoring. The instruction that came back was not *investigate* — it was *observe and document*. Specifically: watch for anyone leaving the shop who subsequently demonstrates anomalous capability in public. The parlor itself is not to be touched. The Artist is not to be spooked. The operation is passive.

**The mechanism:** A fixer named Dex's-not-Dex — the party will never learn his name — received an anonymous contract via a sanitised drop. Instructions: place two or three Maelstrom members near the parlor exit, give them a simple provocation script, report what the marks do. The Maelstrom crew think they're running a stress-test job for a corpo rival checking if a new employee cracks under pressure. They do not know they're proxies for Arasaka. They are not going to ask.

**The documentation apparatus:** There is a drone running a lazy loop over the block. Standard Watson coverage — it wouldn't merit a second look. What it is actually doing is running high-resolution behavioural capture on the alley outside the parlor. If the party uses magic, that footage gets flagged automatically and pulled into the Threat Anticipation queue within forty minutes. A junior analyst cross-references it against the Badlands incident reports — the tomb, the Arasaka response, the anomalous readings from the field team. The cross-reference hits.

At that point the party stops being an anomaly in a tattoo shop's client list and becomes a named file.

> *GM note: nothing happens immediately. The escalation is invisible to the party tonight. What changes is the level at which they're now being considered. Before: a logged curiosity. After: a priority-flagged open case with their faces, build estimates, and a notation that they may have been in the Badlands tomb. The consequences arrive later — and when they do, the party will not know why tonight was the night everything shifted.*

---

## Setup

The party leaves the parlor. Watson above-street is its usual density — traffic noise, food vendors, the particular quality of light that comes from ten competing neon sources at different heights. The alley that runs alongside the parlor staircase is the natural exit path.

Three Maelstrom members are positioned there. They have been waiting for approximately twenty minutes. They are bored in the specific way of people who are trying to look like they belong somewhere they don't.

They are not subtle. They are not supposed to be.

---

## The Maelstrom Crew

**What they actually are:** mid-tier muscle, not soldiers. Cybered moderately — one has a blade forearm mod, one has subdermal plating visible at the neck, the third is the one doing the talking and appears relatively unmodified, which usually means the modifications are internal. They are not incompetent. They are also not prepared for what the party might be.

**What they know:** Stop some people leaving a parlor. Rough them up enough to see how they respond. Report back. Get paid.

**What they don't know:** Who hired the fixer. What the party is. That there's a drone overhead.

**The script, roughly:**

> *"Nice neighbourhood to be wandering through. You look like you've got somewhere to be."*

If the party moves to go around:

> *"Nah, see, I think you've got something in there that belongs to us. Wallet, maybe. Jewellery. Something you picked up on your way in."*

It doesn't have to make logical sense. It's a pretext. The point is provocation.

One of them — the one with the blade mod — has the flat affect. Not hostile-flat. Just *empty*. Moving through the motions of a mugging without apparent investment in the outcome. He's the Five of Pentacles on legs: still functional, still present, already somewhere else. The party may notice. They may not. File it away.

---

## The Branch

### If the party handles it without magic

Standard combat or intimidation. The Maelstrom crew are competent but not exceptional. A clean non-magical takedown reads on the drone footage as *"unusual combat capability, possible military or merc background, not confirmed anomalous."* Gets logged. Does not escalate. The file on the parlor gets a footnote.

This is the safe choice, and the party probably knows it — the Artist's warning is recent. Playing it straight is playing it smart.

> *GM note: reward this. No drone flag, no escalation. The party's caution should feel like it mattered. Don't hint that they got away with anything — just let the scene end cleanly and move on. The absence of consequence is itself information.*

### If the party uses magic

The drone's behavioural capture system flags the anomaly in real time. The Maelstrom crew's reaction — shock, retreat, a dropped weapon — is secondary. What matters is the footage.

Within forty minutes, a Threat Anticipation analyst in a tower somewhere in the city pulls the clip. Within two hours, it is cross-referenced against the Badlands incident file. By morning, the party has a preliminary profile: three to five individuals, anomalous capability confirmed, possible connection to the Quiet Mile tomb event, soul-clear on standard calibration, last seen Watson district.

The party will not know any of this happened.

> *GM note: do not signal the escalation tonight. No ominous drone sound. No suspicious car. Nothing. The horror of this setup is that it looks like a mugging that went fine. The information cost is invisible and already paid. Let them walk into Misty's shop feeling like they handled it.*

---

## What the Crew Does After

Regardless of how the fight ends, the Maelstrom crew breaks off. They were not briefed to press. They report to the fixer: *"Three-to-five, took a run at them, they handled it, couldn't confirm what they're carrying."* The fixer passes it up the chain with a notation about response speed and precision. That's it from the crew's perspective.

The hollow-eyed one is the last to leave. He looks back once. Not a threat — more like a man who has forgotten what he was looking at. Then he goes.

---

## Getting to Misty

The alley exits onto a side street that runs perpendicular to Misty's block. The party can see her shop from here — the sign, the low light in the window, the specific quietness of a ground-floor space in a loud neighbourhood. There's no reason given to go in. It's just there.

If they need a nudge: someone on the street sees them coming out of an altercation and, assuming they're tourists, says *"You want somewhere to sit for a minute, the esoteric place two doors down doesn't ask questions."* Watson hospitality.
