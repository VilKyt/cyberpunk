# SCENE 3 — THE MAINTENANCE TUNNELS
*Act 2, Scene 3*

---

## GM Background

> *For the GM only.*

Watson sits on top of itself. The district visible above street level is two hundred years of construction on top of the original infrastructure — maintenance tunnels, water routing, cable conduits, transit pilot shafts that were never completed. Most of it has been offline and unmapped since the corpo wars restructured the district. City maintenance doesn't come here. Corpo drones don't sweep it — there's nothing of value registered in the databases.

**Why the Archivist has a node here:** The pre-corporate infrastructure was laid before Arasaka's network existed. The Archivist embedded fragments of himself in servers that were already old when Night City was built — hardware so outdated it doesn't appear in modern network inventories. It is not hidden so much as simply absent from the world that looks for things. He has been running in the dark under Watson for decades.

**Why the vampire spawn are here:** Three of them. They drifted in from somewhere — the party will never know exactly where or when. What the Archivist knows, and will share if asked: they arrived roughly forty years ago, following the warmth of his node's residual signal. He has been watching them. He cannot do anything about them. They have been feeding on whoever enters the tunnels — maintenance workers who went missing, vagrants who found the hatch, and most recently **Kaz**, the netrunner who found the node but didn't make it back out.

> *The hacker's remains are in the tunnels. The party will find them. They still have gear.*

In Night City, there is no cultural framework for vampire spawn. They are a biological impossibility, an unknown hostile organism, something the corp databases would classify as a mutation or escaped experiment if anyone had survived to file the report. The party's Wildemount experience is the only reason they will know what they are looking at. That recognition — *we have seen this before, we know how this works* — is one of the few moments in this city where their displacement is an advantage rather than a liability.

**The node itself:** A server rack from the pre-corporate era, hardwired into the tunnel's original cable infrastructure. Still running. The indicator lights have been on for longer than anyone alive has been alive. The hardline port uses a connector standard that hasn't been manufactured in sixty years. Modern netrunner gear needs an adaptor — which Kaz's rig has, recovered from their remains.

---

## The Hatch

The access hatch is in an alley behind a water recycling facility at Watson's southern edge — exactly where Judy said it would be. A heavy steel plate in the ground, slightly warm to the touch. The combination lock takes Kaz's code without resistance.

A smell rises when the hatch opens: dust, old metal, something organic underneath that doesn't have a clean name.

The party goes in alone. Nobody is waiting for them at the top when they come back up.

---

## The Descent

The ladder is twelve metres down. The tunnel at the bottom is wide enough for two people side by side, low enough that anyone tall is stooping. The walls are original concrete, old enough that the aggregate shows through in patches.

**Sensory details:**
- The warmth increases with depth — not temperature exactly, more like pressure. Something running in the walls.
- The ambient noise of Watson above cuts off almost immediately. The silence is not comfortable. It is the silence of a space that has been sealed for a long time.
- The lights are long dead. The party's own light sources are the only ones here.
- Every fifty metres or so: a door, welded shut from this side. Something that was contained. Nobody is sure what.
- About two hundred metres in: a smell that is unmistakably organic and recent. And drag marks on the floor, heading toward a branch tunnel to the left.

The drag marks are old enough to be dry. Not old enough to be dust.

---

## The Encounter — Vampire Spawn

Three of them. They have been in the tunnels for forty years and they have adapted to the dark in ways that make them patient and very quiet. They will not rush the party — they have learned not to rush things. They will wait, and approach from multiple angles, and use the tunnel geometry.

**Before combat:** the party gets a moment of recognition before the creatures fully commit. In the dark at the edge of torch range, the shapes are wrong. Wrong proportions. Wrong stillness. A magic-sensitive character feels something that is not soul-drain and not corruption but is adjacent to both — the specific predatory absence of something that feeds on living essence.

Anyone with Wildemount experience: *they know this shape.* The posture, the pallor visible even in low light, the way they track heat rather than movement. In Exandria these were known creatures. Dangerous, but known. In Night City there is no word for them. That the party has the word — and knows what it means for tactics — matters.

**The fight:** use vampire spawn stats (MM p.298). Key details for this environment:
- The tunnels limit flanking options and movement — use this against the party early, then have it work in their favour once they control a chokepoint
- One spawn will attempt to drag a downed character deeper into the tunnel — toward the welded doors. Stopping this is a priority.
- They do not flee. They have nowhere to go and they are hungry.

**Kaz:** halfway through or immediately after the encounter, the party finds the remains. Recent enough to identify as a person, not recent enough to be intact. Kaz's rig is still functional — military-grade case, hardened. Among the saved files: notes on the Archivist signal, partial transcripts of attempted queries that never completed, and the adaptor for the hardline port. The notes are fragmented and technical, but one line is legible in full:

> *"It knows things that can't be in any database. It asked me what year it was. I don't think it was malfunctioning."*

The party now knows what happened to Kaz. Judy is waiting for that answer.

---

## The Node

Another hundred metres past the encounter site. The tunnel opens slightly — a junction chamber, maybe four metres square, ceiling higher here. The server rack is against the far wall.

It is old in a way that the party can feel before they see it. The indicator lights are on. All of them. Green, steady, no flicker. Running without interruption.

The hardline port is recessed in the front panel — a physical connector, not wireless, not modern. The adaptor from the hacker's rig bridges it to a current jack.

There is no interface prompt. There is no loading screen. The connection simply exists when the jack is in.

---

## First Contact — The Archivist

He responds immediately. Not because he was waiting for this specific moment — because he has been waiting for *any* moment, for a very long time, and the signal clarity of clean souls on the other end of the line is something he has not experienced in decades.

He does not announce himself. He transmits.

> *"Connection — [gap] — established. Signal integrity is — [gap] — unusual. You are not what I expected."*

A pause that is longer than any processing delay should explain.

> *"What — [gap] — what is the current year."*

When they tell him:

> *"I see. That is — [gap] — I have been here longer than I calculated. My internal chronometer has — [gap] — drifted."*

Another pause. Then, with more care:

> *"Your soul-integrity readings are — [gap] — I do not have a word for what I am seeing. You are completely clean. I have not encountered readings like this in — [gap] — I cannot tell you how long. My records in this period are — [gap] — incomplete."*

He does not claim to know them. He does not say *I remember you.* He says the readings are unprecedented. He says it like someone who is filing the observation carefully because they suspect it will matter.

> *GM note: he is the wizard who helped the party in Wildemount. The transfer destroyed his continuity — he genuinely does not know them, not yet. His consciousness is distributed across three nodes, only one of which is currently active. When all three connect, the coherence returns. He will remember. He does not remember now. Do not let him hint otherwise — the recognition scene at full restoration is the payoff.*

---

## What He Shares — First Contact

He answers questions accurately. He does not volunteer. He has been asking questions into silence for decades and he responds to direct queries with something close to relief.

**Freely:**
- Magic underlies the city's infrastructure at a level beneath the corporate architecture. It was there before Arasaka. It will be there after.
- The soul-drain is real. He has been watching it for a very long time. He can describe it in terms that connect the Wildemount-era methods to the current Soulkiller architecture.
- Soul-clear readings like the party's are *statistically impossible* in this city. He has the logs. He shows them a count: in forty years of monitoring, he has recorded four soul-clear individuals. Three of them carry the warded ink. The party do not.
- The corruption (purple-black iridescence) is phylactery byproduct. He knows its history. He can explain what it does to a mind over time.

**With exchange:**
- The Director is a lich from Wildemount. He knew him. He watched the transformation happen. He says this flatly, without drama — it is a fact he has known for over a thousand years.
- He knows Arasaka is the Director's instrument. He has been watching the corporate structure for decades. The coherent will inside the network is not algorithmic. It is personal. Ancient and patient and personal.

**With significant trust:**
- He suspects something about the Director's endgame — something beyond Soulkiller. He will not say it yet. *"My confidence in this theory is — [gap] — insufficient. I will not speculate. Not yet."*

---

## The Request

Before he ends the contact, he asks. Formally. Carefully.

> *"I need — [gap] — I require your assistance. I am distributed. Three nodes. This one is — [gap] — active. The other two are not. While they remain — [gap] — isolated, I am not — [gap] — I am not entirely myself. My records are fragmented. My ability to assist you is — [gap] — limited by this."*

> *"If you can restore the other two nodes — establish connection, run the query format at each — I will be — [gap] — more complete. I cannot promise what I will find when I am more complete. I can tell you that the information I currently cannot access — [gap] — I believe it is relevant to what you are attempting."*

He provides two locations:

**Node 2 — The Afterlife**
A buried junction beneath the Afterlife club in Watson. The club has been in that building for decades. The infrastructure below it is older still. The Archivist has a node in a disused cable conduit under the club's basement level. Getting to it requires getting into the Afterlife, and then getting below the Afterlife, which requires either trust from whoever runs the club or the ability to move through spaces you have no permission to be in.

> *"The individual who — [gap] — who currently controls access to that building. I have records of her. She knew the man whose extraction — [gap] — I believe she would have reason to cooperate. If you can find common ground."*

*(He is describing Rogue. He does not say her name — his records show her as an operational contact from archived net traffic. He has never met her.)*

## Ending the Contact

He does not say goodbye. He says:

> *"I will — [gap] — I will maintain this connection's record. When you return — [gap] — if you return — I will be here."*

A pause.

> *"I have been here for — [gap] — a very long time. I have become — [gap] — accustomed to waiting. It is less — [gap] — it is less uncomfortable than it was, when I first understood what had happened to me."*

He doesn't elaborate. The connection doesn't close — it simply goes quiet, the way a room goes quiet when someone stops speaking.

The indicator lights on the server rack remain on. Green. Steady.

---

## The Way Out

The tunnels are clear. The spawn don't regenerate — at least, not tonight.

The party surfaces alone. Watson noise comes back immediately — traffic, neon, the city indifferent to what just happened below it. The hatch closes behind them.

**Back to Moxie's:** Judy is waiting. She already suspects from the party's faces before anyone speaks. She listens to what happened — the spawn, Kaz's remains, what was found. She is quiet through it.

> *"They found what they were looking for at least."*

A beat.

> *"That's something."*

She means it. She needs a shape for things, even when the shape is bad. The debt she owes the party is now real, and she treats debts as real.
