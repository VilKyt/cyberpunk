# SCENE 1 — THE TATTOO PARLOR
*Act 2, Scene 1*

---

## GM Background — What Is Actually Happening Here

> *For the GM only.*

The tattoo artist — call them **the Artist** until you name them at the table — has been doing ordinary work for twenty years. About a decade ago, a contact they trusted gave them a formula for a specific ink compound. The contact said only: *"Use this on people you'd trust with your life. It won't hurt them. It will make certain things slide off them."* The Artist didn't ask for a fuller explanation. In Watson, you learn not to.

**What the ink does:** encodes a passive abjuration ward into the skin. Anyone tattooed with it reads as soul-clear on Arasaka calibration equipment — the soul-drain's passive signal doesn't register on them. To the Soulkiller network they are effectively invisible. They don't know this. The Artist doesn't know this. They just know the people they've inked seem to move through the city with less friction, stay sharper longer, don't get that specific flat affect that settles into most Night City residents after a decade.

**The contact who gave them the formula:** a magic user, now gone to ground. Two years of silence. The Artist has a dead-drop address — an old messaging protocol the contact set up before vanishing. They haven't used it. They're not sure they want to know why the contact stopped responding.

**The hacker:** about six months ago, a netrunner showed up asking strange questions about the ink, the network, and something they called *"old coherence in the infrastructure."* They mentioned a signal — an AI fragment, pre-corporate, buried under Watson. Called it the Archivist. Left a query format with the Artist before disappearing into the net. The Artist filed it away. They thought it was too dangerous to use alone.

**The protected network:** the Artist has used the special ink on roughly fifteen to twenty people over the years — clients they trusted, people who seemed like they needed it without knowing why. Splice is one. The others are scattered across Watson and the fringe. None of them know each other as a group. This is not an organisation. It's one person quietly trying to do something right in a city that makes that very difficult.

---

## Getting There

Splice knows the address. She doesn't call ahead — that's not how this works. You show up during business hours and you wait.

The parlor is **below street level**, accessed via a staircase off a side alley in Watson. The sign is small and doesn't give a name — just a stylised needle over an eye, faded enough to be almost illegible. The door is reinforced but not conspicuously so.

Watson above is loud. Below the street, it is very quiet.

---

## Beat 1 — The Read

The Artist reads people the way some people read text. Before they say a word to the party, they will look at them for long enough to be slightly uncomfortable. Then:

> *"Splice told you about me."*

Not a question.

They clock the party as unusual immediately — not because of anything they say, but because anyone with calibration sensitivity (or a magic-sensitive player) will notice that the Artist's **own tattoos read faintly as abjuration**. They inked themselves. They didn't know what they were doing when they did it, exactly. They followed the formula. They trusted the contact.

**What they offer at neutral trust:**
- Yes, Splice's tattoo is special
- No, they won't explain how
- Yes, they can do the same for the party, if the party can give them a reason

They are not hostile. They are careful. There is a difference.

---

## Beat 2 — What Trust Unlocks

Trust is earned through straight dealing, demonstrated competence, or shared exposure to risk. They do not respond to threats or manipulation — they shut down, go polite and flat, and wait for the party to leave.

### Low Trust
- The ink is a formula they were given
- They don't know exactly what it does
- They use it on people they'd trust with their life
- They won't say who gave them the formula

### Medium Trust
*(After the party has shown they understand the stakes — that they came out of a tomb, that they can see what others can't, that they're already inside something bigger than them)*
- The contact who gave them the formula has been silent for two years
- They think the contact might be in hiding. Or dead. They don't say *dead* easily.
- Magic — and here they pause before using the word, like they're checking the room — exists in this city. The Artist is certain of it. They've been certain for years. They have no proof they could show anyone.
- It is extremely dangerous to practice. They know of three people who showed abilities in the open. Two disappeared. One is alive and has not been seen in Watson since.
- They use the term **"the quiet stuff"** for anything arcane. As in: *"Your friend had enough of the quiet stuff in her to take the ink."*

### High Trust
*(After something significant — the party protects them, shares real information, or demonstrates that they are as soul-clear as Splice)*
- They'll share the dead-drop address for the contact who gave them the formula
- They'll quietly offer the party the warded ink, with this caveat: *"If something is looking for you specifically, this won't hide you from it. It just means the city stops passively eating you."*
- They don't know exactly where the node is — but they know who does.

A netrunner named **Kaz** came to them some time back asking about the ink, left the query format, mentioned heading into Watson's maintenance infrastructure to find something old that was still listening. Then stopped coming around. The Artist doesn't know what happened to them. But Kaz mentioned a contact before they left — someone they worked with on braindance equipment and underground mapping in Watson.

> *"Her name's Judy. She's good people, does BD tech work out of Moxie's basement — the Moxes club, down near the Kabuki border. Kaz gave her the location details as backup, in case anything went sideways."*

They'll give the party directions to Moxie's. They will not accompany them.

---

## The Question About Magic

At some point the party will ask directly. The Artist's answer, roughly:

> *"In this city? You don't call it that. You don't call it anything. You do it quietly, in rooms with no windows, and you stop if you hear a drone. The corps have something that finds it — not perfectly, not always, but well enough. The people who've lasted are the ones who figured that out early. The ones who didn't..."*

> *[pause]*

> *"Watson has a lot of people who used to have neighbours."*

This is not an explanation. It is the truth of someone who has lived adjacent to it for a decade.

---

## Surveillance Pressure

Watson is not the most heavily surveilled district. It is surveilled enough.

If the party arrived visibly — in a vehicle flagged from the Badlands, in numbers, with magic used in transit — there may be a passive clock running. Arasaka's network doesn't know what it's looking for yet. But anomalies get logged.

**Optional complication:** midway through the conversation, the Artist glances at their phone. Sets it face-down. Continues talking. If pressed: *"Drone sweep. Fifteen minutes, maybe twenty. It's routine. Usually."*

Whether it's routine this time is up to the GM.
