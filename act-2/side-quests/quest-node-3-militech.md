# SIDE QUEST — NODE 3: THE MILITECH FACILITY
*Act 2 side quest. Follows Scene 3 — The Maintenance Tunnels.*

---

## GM Background

> *For the GM only.*

Militech's presence in Watson is not advertised. They leased an industrial building on the district's eastern edge fifteen years ago under a shell company name — officially a logistics hub for a construction supply chain, which handles the occasional delivery that actually arrives. The building's real function: a monitoring station for old pre-corporate infrastructure in the area, part of an ongoing Militech survey of Night City's buried utility networks. They were trying to understand what Arasaka understood about the city's foundations.

They didn't find what Arasaka was looking for. They found enough to know something was down there, filed inconclusive reports, and maintained the station at minimum staffing because closing it would mean admitting the project failed.

**The node:** The Archivist embedded a server fragment in the cable conduit running under the facility's subbasement — pre-corporate infrastructure that predates the building by decades. The facility was built on top of it. Militech's survey equipment has logged the server's ambient signal as an anomaly for years and has never been able to account for it. It's in their reports. Nobody has followed up because nobody knows what it is.

**The defenses:** The facility is not a combat base. It is a monitoring station with security appropriate for protecting expensive equipment from thieves. That said:
- Exterior: two rooftop drones (standard patrol pattern, 90-second gap in coverage at the northeast corner), two guards on rotating exterior sweep
- Interior: four soldiers (contracted, professional), a third drone on interior patrol
- Subbasement: one heavy mech — a Militech Basilisk unit, automated, deployed to protect the survey equipment from whatever the anomalous readings might eventually produce. It has been running for three years without incident. It is bored in the mechanical sense of a system that has had nothing to do.
- The node itself is behind a welded maintenance door in the subbasement corridor, accessible once the mech is handled

---

## Branch A — The Deal

**Getting to Militech:** This requires finding a contact — not Militech directly, but someone who can make an introduction to an operative who has the clearance to make decisions. Options depending on what the party has established:
- Through Rogue (she knows a Militech liaison, will provide an introduction for the right price or if the party has earned it)
- Through the notoriety circuit (a fixer who works both sides of the Arasaka-Militech rivalry can broker a meeting)
- Cold approach: find the shell company's address, send a message up the chain, wait

**The Militech operative:** Mid-level intelligence officer — they deal in information and they are tired of Arasaka winning. When the party shows up with an offer to share what they know about Arasaka's soul-drain operation, the operative's reaction is professional interest over visible scepticism.

They will not simply take the information and grant access. They will:
1. Ask specific questions to establish credibility — what Arasaka is doing to people's minds, what the anomalous signal in their subbasement actually is, why the party wants access
2. Negotiate: they want something in return beyond information — a standing agreement that if the party uncovers further Arasaka intelligence, it comes to Militech first
3. If the party can demonstrate the soul-drain mechanism — showing the corruption on a known Arasaka asset, or the reading difference between a branded agent and a soul-clear individual — the operative's scepticism collapses. This is what they've been looking for an explanation for.

**What Militech provides:** Escort through the facility. The mech is deactivated. A Militech tech opens the maintenance door. The party accesses the node. The tech watches, does not understand what they're seeing, files a report that will confuse their superiors for months.

**The risk:** Militech now knows about magic, the soul-drain, and the party's investigation. They are a rival corp, not an ally. They will use this information in whatever way serves Militech. That may occasionally align with the party's interests. It may not.

> *GM note: the deal branch makes the Militech notoriety track relevant as a parallel system. A party that deals with Militech starts accumulating Militech attention — not hostile at first, but present. They become an asset Militech wants to manage.*

## Branch B — Punch/Stealth

**Getting in:**

*Stealth route:* The northeast corner drone gap (90 seconds) is the entry window. The exterior guards' rotation has a predictable overlap point at the building's south entrance — both guards are briefly at the same corner. The party has about forty seconds. The interior drone's pattern can be tracked through the building's external camera feeds if anyone can pull them (DC 14 Electronics/netrunner check, or physical observation over two patrol cycles).

*Direct route:* The building is not a fortress. It can be hit. The drones go down fast. The guards are professionals but not expecting something like the party. The noise will flag the monitoring system and Militech will know the facility was breached — they won't know by whom, initially.

**The Basilisk:**

The mech in the subbasement is the significant encounter. Militech Basilisk — heavy frame, twin rotary cannons, hardened targeting systems, designed to hold a corridor indefinitely. It has been running on automated patrol in a twenty-metre subbasement for three years. Its targeting software is slightly degraded. Its weapons are not.

*Combat:* The corridor geometry is the key — the Basilisk can't turn fast in a tight space. The maintenance door the party needs is behind it and to the left. Getting to the door means getting past the mech or taking it down.

*Magic:* The Basilisk's targeting systems track heat, motion, and mass. A spell that manipulates any of these — illusion, translocation, gravity effects — can create openings that straight combat can't. The Archivist's dunamancy, if the party has any of his knowledge, is particularly effective: *Immovable Object* on the mech's tread system, *Gravity Sinkhole* pulling it off its patrol path.

*The mech going down:* When it falls, the facility's alarm triggers. The party has twelve minutes before an armed Militech response arrives at the building. They can use those twelve minutes to access the node, run the query format, and get the contact. They cannot afford a longer conversation.

> *GM note: the stealth route avoids the alarm. The direct route creates a clock. Either way, the party gets to the node. The difference is how much time they have with the Archivist before they need to move.*

## At the Node

The server fragment here is the smallest of the three — a secondary relay, just enough hardware to maintain signal coherence. When the query format runs, the Archivist's response is immediate.

> *"Node — [gap] — three. Connection — [gap] — I can feel the structure now. The three points are — [gap] — I am — [gap] — I am more than I was."*

A longer pause than usual.

> *"There is something I need to tell you. I have been — [gap] — I have been waiting to be certain before I said this. I am now — [gap] — sufficiently certain."*

He does not say what it is. Not yet. The session ends. The information is in the restoration scene.

> *GM note: if the party is on the stealth/direct route and has a clock running, the Archivist senses it. He says: "Go. We will — [gap] — we will finish this conversation when you are safe." He prioritises their survival over his own disclosure. This is characteristic.*

---

## Full Restoration — The Recognition Scene

When all three nodes are connected and the party returns to any node for a full conversation, the Archivist is different. The gaps are shorter. The language is more continuous. And then:

> *"I have been — [gap] — I have been reviewing what I remember. The restoration of the three nodes has — [gap] — returned access to records I did not know I still had."*

> *"I remember — I remember —"*

A pause unlike the processing pauses. This one is not mechanical.

> *"I remember you."*

What he says next depends on what the party's relationship with him was in Wildemount — what he helped them with, what he knew of them, what he tried to warn them about. The GM should draw from the established campaign history. Whatever the party shared with this wizard, he has been carrying it in a part of himself that was too fragmented to reach until now.

> *"I did not know you were here. I did not — [gap] — I did not know I was here, not entirely, until this moment. I have been — [gap] — I have been waiting for someone. I did not know it was you I was waiting for."*

**What he gives them at full restoration:**
- His dunamancy knowledge, specifically as it applies to the Soulkiller network's architecture — the theoretical basis for how to disrupt a distributed phylactery at scale
- "The threshold" — what happens when the Director reaches full Soulkiller deployment, the thing he has refused to say until he was certain. It is worse than the party expects.
- His name. He has not said his name in centuries. He says it now.
