# SIDE QUEST — NODE 2: THE AFTERLIFE
*Act 2 side quest. Follows Scene 3 — The Maintenance Tunnels.*

---

## GM Background

> *For the GM only.*

The Afterlife is the most exclusive bar in Night City — legendary, by merc standards. Named for the dead. Entry-level drinks are named after dead legends. Getting in requires either money, reputation, or an introduction from someone already inside. It is not aggressively exclusionary. It simply does not need to try.

Rogue Amendiares runs it. She has run it for years. She was the best solo in Night City before she became the woman who runs the bar where the best solos drink. She knew Johnny Silverhand — knew him in the way you know someone you fought beside and watched die. She has not talked about him recently, not to anyone who would survive reporting back.

**The node:** The Archivist's second server fragment is in a disused cable conduit two levels below the club's basement — original Watson infrastructure, pre-corporate, undocumented on any current city survey. The club was built on top of it. Rogue doesn't know it's there. The staff don't know it's there. A maintenance door in the basement leads to a utility shaft, which leads to the conduit, which leads to the server.

Getting there requires:
1. Getting into the Afterlife (reputation threshold, or Rogue's permission)
2. Getting into the basement (staff-only, requires trust or stealth)
3. Getting into the utility shaft (physical access, the lock is old and not electronic)
4. Running the query format at the node

**Why it matters beyond the node:** The Afterlife is a repository of Night City's living memory. Rogue has been there for most of it. And the Archivist's note about her — *"she knew the man whose extraction—"* — means there is a conversation available here that the party will not be able to have anywhere else.

---

## Getting In

The Afterlife is not impossible to enter. It is meritocratic in the Night City sense: reputation, money, or a compelling reason. The party arriving from the Badlands fringe with no Night City track record starts near the bottom of that list.

**The primary route — notoriety:** Complete 2 of the 3 gigs in [Gigs — Afterlife Access](gigs-afterlife-access.md). Word travels. The door staff will have heard the party's name before they arrive. Entry becomes a professional courtesy rather than a favour. Complete all 3 and the bartender has a drink poured before they reach the bar.

**Alternative routes (if the party skips the gigs):**

**Via Rogue directly:** If the party can get a message to her — through a fixer, through a contact who knows someone — she will grant entry for a meeting if they can demonstrate they know something worth her time. A cold approach with no track record is harder but not impossible.

**Via a contact:** Rogue's bartender knows people. The Afterlife's regulars talk. Anyone with Night City credibility who can vouch for the party works as a bypass — Judy, if the party has established enough trust at Moxie's, can make a call.

---

## Rogue

She is not immediately useful. She is immediately interesting.

She will read the party the same way she reads everyone who walks in: threat level, motivation, whether they're going to be a problem. She will not be impressed by the things people usually try to impress her with.

**What makes her pay attention:**
- They know something about Silverhand. Not the legend — something specific.
- They've been in the Quiet Mile. She has heard rumours. She has not investigated.
- They can see the corruption. If they demonstrate this, or describe it accurately enough that she knows they've seen it — she will go still the way people go still when something they suspected turns out to be confirmed.

**What she will share (staged by trust):**

*Low trust / first meeting:*
- The Afterlife is not a place for tourists
- She's heard the name Silverhand more times in the last month than in the last five years. She doesn't say why. She's logging it.

*Medium trust:*
- She knew Johnny. She doesn't elaborate. The present tense is deliberate and she watches to see if they catch it.
- She has seen corp activity in Watson that doesn't match any job she's aware of — passive surveillance, the kind that isn't looking for something, it's *documenting* something. It started months ago.

*High trust / significant exchange:*
- She ran the original tower raid with Silverhand. She survived. He didn't. She has been waiting for thirty years to understand why the extraction worked — why the chip held when every previous test had failed. She has a theory she will not say aloud in a bar.
- If the party can give her a framework — the Director, the Soulkiller lore, what the party knows — she files it against thirty years of accumulated suspicion and starts drawing conclusions faster than anyone expects. She's been watching this city long enough that she only needed the missing piece.
- She will let the party into the basement. She will not explain to her staff what they're doing there. She will expect a report when they come back up.

## Below the Afterlife

The basement is functional and unglamorous — storage, the building's mechanical systems, a locked room nobody opens anymore. The maintenance door in the far corner has not been opened in years; the manual lock requires a key that Rogue has somewhere, or a pick, or a crowbar if the party isn't feeling patient.

The utility shaft beyond it is narrow. Single-file. Smells of old copper and something that might be ozone. The conduit at the bottom branches three ways; the Archivist's node is at the end of the left branch, behind a section of wall panelling that has been very carefully replaced and can be spotted by someone looking for it.

The server here is smaller than the Watson tunnel node — a secondary fragment, less hardware. But the query format works. The connection establishes. The Archivist's response is different this time — cleaner, faster, as if the second node has already increased his coherence.

> *"Node — [gap] — two. Connection confirmed. I can — [gap] — I can remember more now. Not everything. But — [gap] — more."*

He does not say what he remembers. Not yet. He asks about the third node. His voice in the connection is slightly less fragmented than it was in the tunnels — the gaps shorter, the language a little more continuous.

He knows Rogue is upstairs. He says so.

> *"The woman in the building above. She — [gap] — she was present at an event I have records of. If she is still — [gap] — if she trusted the man whose extraction I have been tracking, she may be — [gap] — she may be someone worth talking to. You may already have. I mention it because — [gap] — because the extraction changed what was possible. And I think she knows that. I think she has known for a long time."*

## Lore Available Here

If the party asks questions while connected at this node, the Archivist's answers are more complete than they were at Node 1. Use this opportunity to deliver lore that requires slightly more coherence:

- The Silverhand extraction in detail — what made it work, what the Director has been building toward since
- The nature of the Soulkiller network's architecture: it runs on the soul-drain infrastructure the Director has been building for centuries, but Soulkiller is the *extraction* mechanism, not the drain itself. The drain feeds the network. Soulkiller is what lets him *keep* specific souls rather than just deplete them
- The warded ink: he has recorded four soul-clear individuals in forty years. Three of them carry ink with a signature he recognizes as abjuration. He would like to know where the formula came from. *"The specificity of the ward suggests someone who understood what they were protecting against. That level of knowledge — [gap] — that is not what I would expect to survive."*
- The Director's known endgame components: the Soulkiller network, a working phylactery distributed at continental scale, and something the Archivist calls *"the threshold" — a coherence level he has not reached yet. What happens at the threshold, the Archivist will not say yet. Not enough nodes.*
