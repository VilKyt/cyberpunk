# GIGS — AFTERLIFE ACCESS
*Three notoriety-building jobs. 2 of 3 gets the party through the Afterlife door.*

---

## Overview

The Afterlife doesn't advertise entry requirements. It doesn't need to. The door staff know who matters in Night City and who doesn't. The party arrives from the Badlands fringe with no track record — they don't exist yet.

That changes with work. Three jobs are available in Watson and the surrounding districts that, done visibly and done well, will put the party's name in circulation among the fixers and mercs who drink at the Afterlife. Complete any two of the three and Rogue's door staff will have heard of them. The third, if they do it, is extra — it earns a warmer reception rather than just entry.

These are not Rogue's jobs. They are standard fixer work available through Night City's underground circuit. The reputation they generate is organic. Word of mouth. The right people talking to the right people.

---

## GIG A — THE RACE

**Source:** Takemura's cousin's rival's fixer — it doesn't matter. Someone who runs Night City's underground circuit puts the word out.

**The job:** Enter a night race through Watson's industrial corridor. Three laps, multiple vehicles, no rules enforced past the start line. The circuit runs through active streets — traffic is a factor, not a problem to be avoided.

**What makes it a notoriety gig:** The field includes established Night City names — people who are known, who will talk afterward. Placing first, or winning in a way that gets remembered (dramatic overtake, handling a deliberate ram attempt, that moment in lap two where someone's car loses a door), puts the party in conversations at the Afterlife bar within forty-eight hours.

**Complications:**
- One of the other drivers has been paid to force a specific car off the road — the party might not know this until it happens to them or someone else
- The route passes within two blocks of an Arasaka checkpoint. Brief, survivable, but the party will need to make a call about whether to show their faces or route around it
- Post-race, the losing fixer tries to recover equipment from the winning vehicle. Standard. Easily handled. But it establishes whether the party's word is good under mild pressure.

**The notoriety beat:** A fixer named Emmett — old, fat, permanently stationed at the Afterlife bar — bets on races and loses badly. He was at the finish line. He will tell anyone who sits near him exactly what happened, in detail, with embellishment. His embellishments always make the winner sound better than the reality. This is why his bets keep losing.

## GIG B — THE WAREHOUSE

**Source:** A mid-tier fixer, working through encrypted channels, wants something retrieved from a corporate-leased warehouse on Watson's industrial waterfront.

**The job:** A shipping container in bay 7 holds six crates of military-grade medical supplies — not Arasaka, not Militech, a smaller corp that leases the space. The supplies were diverted from a humanitarian shipment and are sitting in the container while a shell company figures out how to move them without triggering an audit. The fixer's client wants them before that happens.

**Complications:**
- The warehouse has a security rotation. Three guards, one drone, motion sensors on the bay doors. Standard corporate contractor setup — competent, not elite.
- One of the guards is having a very bad night for personal reasons and is distracted in a way that makes them simultaneously easier to avoid and harder to predict. Their pattern breaks in the third hour.
- The crates are heavier than listed. Something else is in there. The fixer didn't mention it because they didn't know, or because they knew and calculated the party would find out after. Either way, the party makes the call about what to do with what they find.

**What's actually in there (GM decision — choose one):**
- A set of prototype cyberware components Militech thought had been destroyed
- Medical records from a Trauma Team operation that implicate a corp executive in something survivable but embarrassing
- A crate of old weapons with pre-corporate serial numbers that the Archivist, if asked later, will recognise as Wildemount manufacture

**The notoriety beat:** The fixer who hired them pays promptly and adds a notation to their client list. That notation gets referenced when a different fixer asks about reliable Watson operators. Three degrees of separation and it reaches someone who drinks at the Afterlife.

## GIG C — THE KILLER

**Source:** Unofficial. A Watson community board posts it as a missing persons cluster. A fixer with a conscience (they exist) picks it up and offers it as a job.

**The job:** Seven people have disappeared from a three-block radius in Watson over the past six weeks. Trauma Team found one of them — barely alive, bled out in a way that took time, hidden in a sub-basement. The fixer wants the responsible party brought in alive if possible, handled if not.

**What's actually happening:**

The perpetrator is a former Arasaka contractor — tech-role, not combat — who began exhibiting symptoms of advanced soul-drain corruption three months ago. The purple-black iridescence is visible in their eyes when the light catches wrong. They are not fully in control of what they're doing. They are also not fully a person anymore, not in the way they were.

They are hunting in Watson because this is where they lived before the corruption took hold. They are returning to familiar patterns. The victims are people they once knew — acquaintances, a former colleague, someone they used to buy coffee from. The targeting is not random. It is the last recognisable thing about them.

**The complication:** Bringing them in alive means the party will be asked, at some point, what to do with someone who is partially a victim of the same system they're fighting. The corruption is visible to magic-sensitive characters. The fixer doesn't know that what they're dealing with is supernatural in origin.

**The capture vs. kill decision:** The fixer wants alive — there's someone at a university who studies aberrant behaviour and would pay well for a live subject. The community wants dead — the disappearances have made this block afraid. The party's choice will be remembered.

**The notoriety beat:** This one travels faster than the others. A killer in Watson is news. The party being the ones who dealt with it is more news. By the time it reaches the Afterlife, the story has evolved considerably. Emmett will be telling a version of it that is twenty percent accurate and entirely compelling.

---

## The Door

Two of the three done — through any combination — and the door staff at the Afterlife will step aside when the party approaches. No words needed. One of them might say: *"Heard about the warehouse job. Come in."*

Three done, and the bartender has a drink poured before they reach the bar.

Either way, they get to Rogue. What happens next is in [Node 2 — The Afterlife](quest-node-2-the-afterlife.md).
