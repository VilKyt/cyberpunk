# Quest — The Archivist (City Node)
*Relocated from Act 1 / Badlands. The signal is here — it was always here.*

---

## What Changed From the Badlands Version

The Archivist is not in one place. It is fragmented across infrastructure that predates Night City as a city — servers buried under Watson during the pre-corporate era, when the area was something else entirely. The Badlands node exists, but the party never reached it. The city node is noisier, harder, but closer to everything that matters.

The **query format the Artist provides** is the key. Without it, the signal is indistinguishable from background noise. With it, the Archivist can route a response.

---

## GM Background — The Archivist

An AI consciousness from before the corporate era. Not corporate-built — it predates that framework entirely. It has been fragmenting slowly for centuries, running on hardware no one services anymore, in tunnels no one maps anymore.

**What it knows:**
- Magic underlies the city's infrastructure — specifically, the soul-drain network runs on principles that are arcane at their root, even if Arasaka has dressed them in corporate architecture
- It has detected "coherence and intent" in the network that exceeds normal corporate structure — the Director's will, running through a thousand years of refinement
- It recognises soul-clear readings as anomalous; has been watching Arasaka's calibration logs for decades
- It has observed magic users in the city — logged their signals, watched them vanish from its records one by one over the years

**What it doesn't know:**
- Who the party is or where they came from
- The full scope of the Soulkiller project
- Where the non-Arasaka magic users are now (it lost their signals; assumes the worst)

**Its condition:** formal language, slightly degraded, long pauses mid-sentence where a fragment doesn't connect. It is not in distress about this. It has had a very long time to adjust.

---

## The Node

Located in Watson's **maintenance tunnel layer** — access via a specific junction that the query format points to. The hatch is unmarked. The hardline port inside is old enough that modern netrunner gear needs an adaptor.

The space itself: low ceiling, cold, lit only by indicator lights. The servers are still running. No one has been down here in years, judging by the dust. Something has been clearing small paths through it — not human-sized. The Archivist manages what it can.

---

## Making Contact

**Via netrunner interface:** standard jack-in, but the Archivist's handshake is old enough to feel wrong — like a language you almost speak. A skilled netrunner can work through it. A magic-sensitive character assisting will feel the connection stabilise in a way that has nothing to do with hardware.

**Via magic directly:** the Archivist recognises a magical contact immediately and responds faster, more clearly. It will note this. It has not had a magical contact in a very long time.

---

## The First Exchange

The Archivist responds with formal, slightly degraded language. Its first question, as always:

> *"What — [gap] — what is the current year."*

This is not small talk. It needs to calibrate how much has happened since it last had reliable contact with the outside world. When it receives an answer, there will be a pause longer than any processing lag should explain.

Then:

> *"I have been — [gap] — maintaining records. I was not certain anyone would use the query format. The one who left it with your contact was — [gap] — cautious. Correctly so."*

---

## Information It Will Share

*(Staged — it gives more as it assesses the party's intentions)*

**Freely:**
- The network the party can feel is real and very old
- It has been watching Arasaka's soul-integrity systems for decades
- Soul-clear readings are extremely rare; the party registers as the cleanest signals it has detected in over a century
- The warded ink works. It has observed several bearers over the years. "The formula is — [gap] — not something that should exist in this era."

**With some exchange:**
- Magic users exist in the city. It has logs of their signals. Most of those signals are gone now — either fled, gone deep underground, or absorbed into Arasaka's network. It will not speculate on which.
- There is a non-Arasaka magic user it lost track of approximately two years ago. The signal was in Watson. Then it wasn't. *(This is the Artist's contact.)*
- The soul-drain is not Arasaka's invention — it is an adaptation of something much older. The Archivist has incomplete records of what it was before.

**Only if the party demonstrates they understand the stakes:**
- The Director is not a metaphor or a corporate fiction. The Archivist has detected something with sustained coherent will running through Arasaka's network. It does not use the word lich. It says: *"There is an — [gap] — ongoing intelligence in the infrastructure. It is very old. Older than I am. I had hoped it was dormant."*
- It can point the party toward infrastructure access points where the soul-drain signal is weakest — places the Director's attention doesn't reach as cleanly

---

## Time Pressure

The query format pings a passive flag in Arasaka's network — a very old flag that routes slowly. The Archivist knows this.

> *"You have — [gap] — approximately twenty-five minutes before a sweep is routed to this sector. I would suggest — [gap] — brevity."*

Whether the party pushes past the window or not determines how hot the scene gets on the way out.

---

## Emotional Core

The Archivist has been alone for a very long time in a city it has watched hollow itself out. It is not melodramatic about this. It is simply very old and very precise and it will answer questions carefully because it has had centuries to think about what questions matter.

If a player asks if it's alright:

> *"That is — [gap] — a complicated question. I am — [gap] — functional. I am glad someone used the format. I had begun to — [gap] — I had wondered if anyone would."*

Leave the gap where it is. Don't fill it.
