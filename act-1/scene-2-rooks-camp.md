# SCENE 2 — ROOK'S CAMP
*Act 1, Scene 2*

---

## GM Background — The Camp's Function

> *For the GM. Sets tone and stakes.*

Rook's camp is not a settlement. It has no name the clan would put on a map — if they had maps, which they don't, because maps are how corps find you. It moves three or four times a year along a loose circuit through the Badlands fringe, always staying beyond the outermost range of Night City's tower sensors.

Right now it's tucked into the lee of a slag ridge about **forty minutes from the Quiet Mile** at full AV pursuit speed, or an hour by ground if you know the back routes. Rook knows the back routes. She drove them in the dark without headlights, which tells you something about how many times she's done this.

The camp is: sixteen vehicles, most of them functional, a few being kept alive by willpower and wire. Canvas stretched between them for shade. Generators running on reclaimed fuel. About **thirty people**, a third of them children or elderly. Working dogs. Mismatched solar panels. The smell of engine grease and woodsmoke and someone cooking something with real chiles.

The party arrives here **still wrong** — not quite at full capacity, the tomb's residue still fading from their systems. This camp is their first real chance to stop, breathe, and figure out what year it is.

---

## Beat 1 — Arrival

The vehicles pull in through a gap in the slag ridge that's invisible until you're almost through it. The nomads who stayed at camp come out to meet them — some tense, some curious, one child who immediately tries to touch the party's weapons until an adult grabs her collar.

Rook parks, kills the engine, and sits for a moment before getting out. She's been running calculations the whole drive.

### The First Hour
Give the players **unstructured time** to react. They've just escaped Arasaka agents, woken up in the wrong millennium, and are in a camp full of strangers who speak a dialect of Common they mostly understand, using words they don't.

**Key environmental details to offer:**
- A flatscreen bolted to the side of a truck running a news feed — footage of the Quiet Mile from a distance, crawl text about a "seismic event near restricted badlands coordinates." No mention of the party. Not yet.
- Someone has a **music player** running something loud and melodic. It's a genre that doesn't exist in Wildemount. Several people are singing along.
- Dogs. Normal dogs. The party's magic does not disturb them the way humans' nerves do. This is quietly the most comforting moment of the session.
- The sky at this hour: Massive, clear at the edges, hazy orange-brown toward Night City's glow on the horizon. A flight lane of commercial AVs, high up, moving in a slow arc. The sheer scale of the city even at this distance.

### Rook's Instruction
She doesn't offer the camp freely. She tells the party:

> *"You can stay until morning. You don't touch the supply caches, you don't go past the east berm, you don't use whatever you can do where my people can see it. Vera will see you're fed. After that we talk."*

She walks away. She does not look back to see if they obey.

**What she's actually doing:** Checking with three different people she trusts about what they saw at the Quiet Mile. Comparing notes. Trying to figure out what she's let into her camp.

---

## Beat 2 — Vera

*[See NPC file: [Vera](../npcs/vera.md)]*

She finds the party before they find her. She's been watching since they arrived.

Vera is mid-fifties, compact, deliberate in her movements — the stillness of someone who has spent decades making sure nothing she does is wasted. She has a prosthetic left forearm — not sleek Arasaka chrome, but a heavy, practical replacement that she maintains herself. A long scar from the right ear to jaw that she makes no effort to hide.

She runs the camp's weapons. That means triage, maintenance, training, and when necessary, deciding who gets what. She is not sentimental about any of it.

### What She Offers
- **Gear assessment:** She will look at what the party is carrying — their Wildemount equipment — and give a flat, honest evaluation. Enchanted weapons read as "weird metal, unusual balance, holds an edge better than it should." She doesn't have words for magic but she knows quality and she knows wrong. She won't panic. She'll want to know where they got them.
- **Trade:** The camp's stockpile is practical — firearms, blades, maintenance equipment. She can trade for things that have value to her. She is not interested in gold. She is interested in working weapons, ammunition in calibres she can use, and anything that gives her camp an edge.
- **Training (if asked or prompted):** Vera will run combat drills for anyone who asks. She charges: labour around camp, or a favour that she'll name later. She does not teach for free because nothing in the Badlands is free. But she is genuinely good, and what she teaches in an hour has kept people alive.

### Her Read on the Party
She knows they're not right. Doesn't know why. She's seen shock, she's seen injury, she's seen people who've been in bad places and come back missing pieces of themselves. The party reads as none of those things — they're *present*, maybe more present than anyone she's met. It bothers her in a way she can't name.

If any player casts anything within her line of sight, she goes very still. Then:

> *"Do that again."*

Not a threat. An instruction. She has seen the purple-black discharge at the Quiet Mile from a distance. She doesn't know what it means. She knows what she just saw the party do is the opposite of that, and she wants to understand the difference.

## Beat 3 — Mote

*[See NPC file: [Mote](../npcs/mote.md)]*

He comes to them. There's a camp medical area — two folding tables, better equipment than you'd expect, very organised — and he's been trying not to be obvious about watching them from it since they arrived.

Mote is late twenties, intense focus, mismatched cyberware that he clearly installed on himself. He introduces himself as the camp's medic. He does not immediately say he also does cyberware installation, because Rook has told him not to lead with it when new people arrive.

### The Conversation
He wants to run a basic medical scan on anyone willing — "after what happened at the Quiet Mile, exposure risk, just a precaution." This is partially true. He is also curious in the way that someone who has spent years studying bodies is curious when they see something they haven't encountered before.

**If the party allows a scan:**
His equipment is calibrated to Night City baseline human physiology, which includes trace levels of ambient soul-drain corruption. The party reads as **anomalous clean** — the same way they appeared on the Arasaka netrunner's scan, but Mote doesn't have the framework to know what it means. He sees:

> *"Your neural interface compatibility is... off the charts, technically, but — it's like the baseline is different. Like you were built to a spec I've never seen. You're healthy. More than healthy. Your cellular cohesion is — I don't have a word for this."*

He's not alarmed. He's riveted. He will ask if he can run more tests. He will keep asking.

### What He Can Do
- **Cyberware consultation:** He can explain the basics of how implants work, what's available at the camp (limited: trauma mods, basic neural interface components, refurbished optical), and what requires Night City supply lines (everything interesting). He frames Night City not as a destination but as a supply chain.
- **Basic installation:** Can safely install low-grade military cyberware on willing subjects. His technique is good. His stock is limited. Nothing from the premium catalogue without a city contact.
- **The warning:** He won't say this outright, but if pressed: the better Arasaka-manufactured implants carry a faint integration layer that he doesn't fully understand. He's pulled them from clients before and found traces of something that isn't in the spec sheet. He assumed it was proprietary DRM. It's not, but he doesn't know that yet.

## Beat 4 — Rook's Debrief

Late in the evening, after the camp has settled, Rook comes to find the party. She brings food — actual food, something with protein, she's not a bad host — and sits across from them without preamble.

### What She Wants to Know

She has three questions she'll ask directly:

1. **"How long were you actually down there."**
   She was not satisfied with whatever answer came at the tomb. She needs a real one. If the party tells the truth — *a thousand years, we're from another world, we don't know how* — she sits with it. She doesn't disbelieve them. She files it.

2. **"What did you hear the Arasaka agent say. Before things went wrong."**
   She wants confirmation of *"souls are clean."* She's heard that phrase before. She won't say where yet.

3. **"Can you do it again. The thing you did at the tomb."**
   She means the magic. She's seen the response it triggered — the agents calling for the Director personally. She wants to know how much of a liability she's carrying.

### What She'll Share

If the party is honest with her, Rook opens a fraction. Not warm. But straight.

> *"My grandmother kept a journal. Old, handwritten, starting about forty years ago. She spent fifteen years of it on one thing — a woman she kept almost finding, out in the Badlands. Said the woman knew things about what Arasaka was doing that no one else did. Said she kept disappearing before anyone could reach her. Last entry just says: 'She's still out there. She's waiting for the right people.'"*

She pauses.

> *"I've been scanning the Quiet Mile for two years. I don't know what I expected to find. Not you."*

**What she won't say:** She's heard "Convergence" before — in a corp transmission she intercepted six months ago. She's not ready to trust the party with that. It's the only leverage she has and she doesn't know yet if they're worth the trade.

### The Archivist Seed
Near the end of the conversation, one of Rook's lookouts comes in to report. She dismisses them with a wave — *"just the signal again"* — but the party catches it if they're paying attention.

If pressed:
> *"Weeks now. Low-frequency broadcast, Badlands origin, southeast of here. Locals think it's salvage bait — a downed ship's transponder, maybe. I've been ignoring it. It doesn't repeat like a transponder. It responds."*

She says this last part flatly. She has not told anyone else. She does not know what to do with it.

---

## Beat 5 — The Decision

Morning. The camp breaks down on schedule — Rook doesn't linger anywhere. The question is what happens to the party.

### Rook's Offer
She will take them to **the Night City fringe** — the outer transition zone where the Badlands start becoming city, where Arasaka's full surveillance grid doesn't quite reach. She can do this without detection because she does it regularly; the clan runs grey-market supply lines through the fringe.

She will not go into the city. She doesn't say why but it's visible — a slight tension in her jaw when she looks at the skyline. The city took something from her. She doesn't owe it another visit.

**The conditions:**
- They don't attract Arasaka response on the drive in
- They don't burn Rook's contacts at the fringe checkpoint
- If they find out what "Convergence" means, they tell her

### What She Gives Them
Rook hands over a **burner comm** — basic, encrypted, one stored contact which is her. And a folded piece of paper with a hand-drawn map. Two locations marked: one is where she's dropping them. The other is unlabelled, southeast. The signal. She doesn't explain the second one. She just gives them the paper.
