# QUEST A — THE WRAITH FORAY
*Act 1 Side Quest. Rook's trust test. Combat-forward.*

---

## GM Background

> *For the GM.*

The Wraiths are not a real faction. They're six people who used to be more and have spent two years becoming less — a splinter group from a clan that dissolved after a bad season, now operating as opportunistic scavengers in the fringe east of Night City. They are not stupid. They are hungry and they have been watching the Quiet Mile event from a distance.

They know a tomb was cracked. They know a pulse of light came out of it. They know Arasaka sent an AV and then the AV left without apparent collection. To them this reads: salvage, abandoned, up for grabs. They've been wrong before but they're out of better options.

Rook knows they're coming because she has a lookout rotation that's been running for six months. She's not scared of the Wraiths. She is unwilling to put her people — most of whom are not fighters — in front of six armed scavengers when she has potentially useful strangers currently eating her food.

This is as much an audition as a request.

---

## Setup

Rook comes to the party in the early afternoon. Not urgently. She sets a tin cup down and sits across from whoever she finds.

> *"Six people, armed, moving toward us from the northeast. They'll be at the perimeter by dusk. They think we pulled salvage from the Quiet Mile. We didn't — you did. I'd like them to leave. My people don't do this."*

She waits. She does not explain what the reward is. If asked, she says:

> *"You've been here a day. You want to know what the reward is for not having six scavengers in my camp. Think about it."*

---

## Beat 1 — Scout Phase

Rook gives them **two hours** before dusk. The Wraiths are still an hour out. The party can use this time to:

- **Observe the approach** — Perception DC 13 from the slag ridge gives a clean view. They can count, assess weapons (pistols, one shotgun, light armour, no armoured vehicle), and identify the formation. Three up front, one flanking left, two holding back near their bikes.
- **Set up** — the camp perimeter has natural chokepoints: a gap in the vehicle ring on the northeast side that Rook's people funnel supply vehicles through. The Wraiths will approach this gap. Cover on both sides. Advantageous ground if the party gets there first.
- **Send a message** — if they want to negotiate before contact, they can approach under a flag. Deception or Persuasion DC 15 to convince the Wraiths to stand down entirely. This is hard: the Wraith leader (see below) doesn't believe the tomb was empty. He'll need proof or a better offer.

---

## Beat 2 — The Fight (or Near-Fight)

**The Wraiths (6):**

| Name | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Carve** (leader) | Shotgun, aggressive | Corruption detail — see below |
| **Two unnamed veterans** | Pistols, experienced | Will follow Carve's lead |
| **Two unnamed opportunists** | Pistols, undisciplined | Will break if Carve goes down or things go wrong |
| **Lena** | Pistol, barely there | Young, scared, in over her head — see below |

**Tactics:** They're not corp-trained. They spread out to avoid being caught in a group, move to cover, shoot at anything that moves. They are not tactically sophisticated but they have local knowledge and they're used to people who back down.

**Turn dynamics:**
- If the fight starts well for the party (two or more Wraiths down in round 1), the opportunists make a Morale check (Wisdom DC 12) or break and run for their bikes.
- If the fight stalls (nobody down after 2 rounds), Carve yells for Lena to circle — she won't, she freezes, and this is the moment a perceptive character notices she's not moving.
- If Carve goes down, the veterans pull back to the bikes. They're not dying for a job.

**Resolving without full combat:** If the party flanked well in Beat 1, or if they drop Carve fast, the remaining Wraiths will accept a withdrawal offer. They're not suicidal.

---

## Beat 3 — Carve

**The corruption detail:** When the party gets close to Carve — during or after the fight — a magic-sensitive character who looks at him directly sees it: the faint purple-black iridescence at the edge of his irises. Not the sharp branded mark of the Arasaka agents from Scene 1. Ambient. Passive. The kind of trace that comes from years of proximity to the city's infrastructure without any corp affiliation or protection.

He doesn't know. He's been in the fringe his whole life. The drain network runs through the pipelines, the relay towers, the dead spots in the signal that people call interference. He's been slowly integrated without consent or awareness for years.

If he's alive after the fight and the party tries to talk to him:
- He's not grateful. He's recalibrating. He'll tell them the tomb was broadcasting for weeks before anyone cracked it. *"Whatever was in there, it was loud. We're not the only ones who heard it."*
- He won't say who else heard it. He doesn't know exactly — just that there were other people with better equipment than his doing sweeps near the Quiet Mile a month ago. Not Arasaka. Someone else.

This detail is a thread the GM can develop or leave. Planted, not required.

---

## Beat 4 — Lena

She's seventeen. She's been with the Wraiths for four months because there was nowhere else. She is not a threat. If the party doesn't kill her (she never fires a shot at anyone), she's standing at the edge of the perimeter when it's over, not running, not advancing, waiting to find out what happens to her.

**Options:**

- **Turn her away:** She goes back to the Wraiths, if they're intact. If Carve is dead and the Wraiths scattered, she has nowhere to go. She'll follow the party at a distance until someone deals with her.
- **Rook takes her:** If the party brings her to Rook, Rook assesses her for about thirty seconds and says *"You can stay three days. After that you earn your spot or you leave."* Lena stays. She's not a named NPC unless the party invests in her.
- **Vera's assessment:** If Vera sees the interaction, she watches without comment. Later she tells whoever brought Lena in: *"She's got a good eye. Wastes it on being frightened."* This is as close as Vera gets to approving of something.

---

## Aftermath — Rook's Trust

Rook doesn't thank them. She does something more useful: she tells them something she wasn't going to tell them yet.

> *"The Convergence transmission I intercepted. It wasn't just the Quiet Mile coordinates. There was a secondary flag — an old file number. I've been trying to trace it. It's Arasaka Internal, forty years old, sealed. But the flag is recent. Someone pulled it from archive six months ago."*

She doesn't have the file contents. She has the flag. The file number: **CVG-0047-INTERNAL**. She gives it to them because they just earned one thing she doesn't give easily: a reason to think they might be worth the risk.

**Trust state:** Cautious → Neutral.
