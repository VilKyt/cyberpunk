# QUEST B — THE TOMB RUN
*Act 1 Side Quest. Supply escort. Rook's practical request.*

---

## GM Background

> *For the GM.*

The tomb contained two categories of things. The first is the party's own gear — Wildemount weapons and equipment, which were in the chamber when they were sealed in and are now available again, reading to modern instruments as unusual alloys with unexplained properties.

The second category is the Director's instruments. Not his primary equipment — he took that when he left. What remained are the peripheral tools of a soul-drain facility: calibration devices, resonance anchors, the physical infrastructure of a process he abandoned and never bothered to clean up. To the naked eye: dense metal objects, pre-corporate age, intricate construction. To a magic user: they feel wrong in the way all his work feels wrong, but inert. The drain cycle stopped when the seal broke. The instruments are empty shells.

To a tech fence in the Badlands: advanced pre-corporate salvage, no serial numbers, no corporate registry, worth significant money to the right buyer.

Rook's contact is **Henk** — a waystation operator and fence about 90 minutes east of camp. She's used him for three years. He's reliable in the specific way that reliable means "hasn't sold her out yet and has financial reasons to maintain that streak." She can't take the run herself: since the Quiet Mile event, Arasaka contractor drones have been doing passes on her vehicle signature. She needs drivers they don't know.

**She asks after Quest A**, if trust is at Neutral. Before that, she doesn't trust them with her contact's location.

---

## The Job

Rook explains it clean:

> *"Henk's waystation. Box of instruments from the tomb — pre-corporate, no markings, nothing that traces back to here. The route takes 90 minutes and goes through Arroyo Gate — old mining settlement at the canyon mouth, only vehicle passage through the ridge. Militech contractors run it now. It's a toll, not a scan. Pay, move through, don't give them a reason to look at the cargo.*
>
> *Don't try to go around. South side's mined — their work, eight years back. North wall's sheer. The road goes through the Gate.*
>
> *There's been a tip-off problem on this corridor for three months. Someone's been flagging unusual cargo to Militech. I don't know who. Keep it in mind — if they're watching for us, the Gate is where they'll act."*

She gives them: a battered utility vehicle (starts on the third try), a cargo box with the instruments sealed inside, the route marked on a hand-copy map, and Henk's frequency for radio contact.

---

## Beat 1 — Preparation

The party has time before departure to examine the cargo and handle any preparation.

**Examining the instruments (Arcana DC 12 for magic users, Investigation DC 15 for non-magic):**

The instruments are cold and inert. They are also recognisable — the same geometry as the drained symbols on the tomb walls, but physical, manufactured. A magic user who handles them for more than a minute makes a Wisdom save DC 10. On a failure: a brief sensation of *pulling*, like the device is testing for something. Then nothing. They are dormant. But they respond to presence.

**The partially-active one:** One instrument, smaller than the others, has a faint residual warmth. Not threatening. When a magic user (or a very clean soul) holds it, it makes a sound — subsonic, below hearing but felt in the chest. Mote, if he's along, notices his medical scanner gives an error reading near it. He files this. His log is getting full.

The party can choose to:
- Leave this one with Mote for study (reduces cargo value but removes the risk of it doing something at Henk's)
- Bring it along (Henk will notice it's different)
- Leave all instruments and abort the run (Rook won't ask again for a while)

---

## Beat 2 — Arroyo Gate

### Why You Can't Drive Around

Forty minutes into the route, the flat Badlands hit a ridge — a long, low escarpment that runs north-south for roughly twelve kilometres with no vehicle-passable gap. Rook's map marks the one exception: **Arroyo Gate**, a pre-corporate mining settlement built at the mouth of a dry canyon where the canyon floor is wide and flat enough for vehicles.

- **North of the road:** the canyon wall is sheer, fractured basalt. On foot with climbing gear, maybe. Not with a cargo vehicle.
- **South of the road:** a rusted Militech warning sign, and beyond it, a flat that looks driveable but isn't. The contractors who took the settlement mined it when they moved in, eight years ago. Some mines are probably still active. The signs are old enough that the skull-and-crossbones has faded, but the word MINE is still legible. Perception DC 12 will spot disturbed earth patterns at the edge of the flat.
- **The flanks:** The contractors installed motion sensors on both shoulders of the approach road, 400 metres out. They go unnoticed unless the party scouts on foot first (Perception DC 15 to spot the sensor poles, which are painted the same rust-orange as the rock). If triggered, the guards inside the town know a vehicle is approaching two minutes before it arrives.

Driving back and finding a different route would add three hours and require Rook's help to reroute through terrain she doesn't know. The road goes through Arroyo Gate.

---

### The Settlement — Layout

A dead mining town, maybe twenty buildings, most collapsed to their foundations. The Militech contractors use four of them. The canyon mouth is roughly 80 metres wide; the main street runs straight through for about 150 metres before the canyon opens back into flat Badlands.

```
[APPROACH ROAD — open flat, 300m visible]
        |
  [MOTION SENSORS — 400m out, flanks]
        |
  [FRONT GATE — barrier, booth, 2 guards]
        |
  [MAIN STREET — 150m, buildings either side]
    Left:  [GUARDHOUSE — bunkroom, 4 off-duty]
    Right: [VEHICLE BAY — 2 bikes, 1 truck]
    Left:  [CAMERA POLE — mid-street]
    Right: [RUINS — good cover, no occupants]
        |
  [BOSS BUILDING — command post, right side near back]

        |
[CANYON EXIT — road continues east toward Henk]
```

### Personnel (9 total)

| Position | Count | Weapons | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Front gate guards** | 2 | Pistols, light armour | Visible on approach. Will call inside if anything feels wrong. |
| **Sniper** | 1 | Rifle (2d10 piercing, range 150/600 ft) | On elevated position, back third of the street. Has line of sight to the front gate and most of the main street. First priority in any fight. |
| **Off-duty in guardhouse** | 4 | Pistols + 1 automatic | Emerge **2 rounds after shooting starts**. Guardhouse is left side, mid-street. They come out the side door — not the street-facing door. |
| **Boss** | 1 | Automatic + armour vest (+2 AC) | In the command building, right side near back. Runs the tip-off operation. If the cameras are still up, he's the one calling it in. |

---

### The Camera System

Three cameras: one on the front gate structure, one on the mid-street pole, one covering the boss building entrance. They feed to the boss's terminal inside.

**If the cameras stay up during a fight:** The boss calls the Militech contractor regional post — response time approximately 25 minutes (one AV, four contractors). This is a hard timer if the party takes too long.

**Disabling the cameras:**
- Netrunner quickhack (Distract Device, 1 RAM): loops a single camera for 1 round. Not sufficient on its own but buys time on a specific angle.
- Physical: shooting a camera is a ranged attack, AC 13, 5 HP. Loud but permanent.
- At the source: the boss's terminal is inside his building. Destroying or hacking it kills the feed entirely. Reaching it requires getting to him first.

---

### Negotiation (Before Things Go Loud)

If the party approaches normally and pays the toll, the front gate guards are professional-indifferent. **Toll: 80 eddies per vehicle.** Standard cargo, no scan. The checkpoint's income is the toll plus the tip-off payments — they're not looking for a fight with paying customers.

If they declare the cargo:
- Guard runs a basic scanner. Returns "dense metal objects, pre-war manufacture." No alert.
- Guard adds a 200-eddy "salvage levy." Invented. Standard shakedown.
- Pay it and they're waved through. Decline and the guard calls into the guardhouse — two more guards emerge at the front gate. Tension, not combat, unless the party pushes further.

**The boss doesn't appear unless shooting starts or someone pushes hard enough to demand to speak to him.** He's the one who runs the tip-off operation — if the party captures him rather than killing him, they can learn the full chain.

---

### If It Goes Loud — Combat Phases

**Round 1:** Front gate guards engage. Sniper acquires a target from elevation — first shot is at advantage (hasn't been spotted yet). Party hopefully has some cover behind their vehicle or the gate structure.

**Round 2:** Off-duty guards emerge from the guardhouse side door (mid-street, left). Boss hears the shooting; starts the camera call if the feed is still up. Back gate guard takes cover but doesn't advance yet.

**Round 3+:** The fight is a street fight through a 150-metre corridor with one elevated shooter making forward movement costly. The vehicle bay (right, mid-street) and the ruins (right, mid-street) provide cover but require crossing open ground from the gate.

**Tactical notes:**
- The sniper is the first problem. He has elevation and a clear field. A party that doesn't deal with him inside two or three rounds will take sustained pressure.
- The guardhouse side door is a chokepoint — the four guards come out in sequence, not simultaneously. The first two are easy to catch in the doorframe if anyone is watching that angle.
- The boss building has reinforced shutters — he's not easy to shoot through the walls.
- Vehicles can be driven through the street as cover or to ram the barriers, but the canyon is narrow enough that a crashed vehicle can block the road in either direction.

**Morale:** The guards are contractors, not true believers. If the sniper goes down *and* three or more others are down, the remaining guards will consider a withdrawal — Persuasion DC 12 to stop the fight entirely. The boss does not surrender easily; he knows what the tip-off chain means for him if Rook finds out.

---

### After the Fight

The settlement is theirs for however long they want it. Useful finds:
- The boss's terminal: tip-off logs, the Militech handler's frequency, proof of the shakedown operation. Rook will want this.
- The vehicle bay: two bikes in functional condition, one truck with a cracked engine block (repairable with 2 hours and a Mechanics/Investigation DC 13 check).
- A small cache of ammunition and two functional pistols in the guardhouse.
- The cameras, if not destroyed, are now an unmonitored feed. Could be turned to face the other direction before anyone checks.

**The timer:** If the camera call went out, the party has a window. Twenty-five minutes is enough to loot and move, not enough to hold the settlement. If the call was stopped, no response is coming — but the Militech regional post will notice the checkpoint went silent eventually. Hours, not minutes.

---

### Who's Tipping Them Off

The boss — a contractor named **Vael** — runs the tip-off operation personally. He has the Militech handler's frequency memorised and a log of every unusual cargo flag he's filed in eighteen months. He's paid per confirmed interception. He doesn't know what Rook's crew moves — only that the runs from the Quiet Mile corridor have been consistently unusual since the tomb event.

If captured and pressed: he gives up the handler's name and frequency without much resistance. He values his life over the operation. He does not know what Arasaka has flagged about the corridor — he's Militech-adjacent, not corp-integrated.

**Rook's reaction:** If the party brings her Vael's log, she goes quiet for a long moment. Then: *"Eighteen months. He's been selling us for eighteen months."* She doesn't say what she does with that information. She does it anyway.

---

## Beat 3 — Henk's Waystation

A refuelled petrol station that died and became something else. Three connected structures, solar panels, a signal jammer on the roof that makes phones unreliable within 30 meters (intentional). Dogs. The smell of old machinery and good coffee.

**Henk** is fifty-three, has been running this waystation for twenty years, and looks like a man who made a decision twenty years ago and has not reconsidered it since. He is practical, not warm. He waves them into the covered bay without ceremony.

**The transaction:**

He runs a scanner over the cargo. Standard stuff. Then the scanner returns an error on the smaller instrument (if the party brought it). He pauses. Runs it again. Puts the scanner down.

> *"This one's alive."*

He's wrong about that — it's dormant, not alive. But it reads differently from everything else. He's seen pre-war tech before. He hasn't seen pre-war tech that responds to the person holding it.

He buys the cargo at agreed rate (Rook's negotiated price: 2,400 eddies for the lot). The partially-active instrument he offers to buy separately for an additional 600 — *"for my own curiosity."*

**What Henk does with information:** He doesn't sell Rook. He does maintain a private log of unusual finds. If the party leaves the active instrument with him, it will eventually draw attention — not immediately, and not from Arasaka directly. But something old and unusual, moving through fringe markets, leaves a trace. This is a slow-burn risk, seeded now.

**What Henk knows about the area:** He's been watching the Quiet Mile signal for fifteen years. He knows the Archivist signal — he calls it *"the grandmother signal"* because an old woman who used to come through his waystation years ago was the one who first told him it responded to queries. She died six years ago. He knows her name.

If the party asks, he tells them: **Mireille Voss**. Watson district. She was 101 when she stopped coming through. He doesn't know she died at 107.

---

## Aftermath — Rook's Trust

Rook checks the vehicle. Checks the take. Doesn't comment on either.

Twenty minutes later she comes back and hands over the cut: 1,600 eddies (she keeps 800 for the camp). And:

> *"Henk's name stays with you. Not your crew, not Splice, not anyone you meet in the city. He's stayed clean because people keep him that way."*

Pause.

> *"You did alright."*

**Trust state:** Neutral → Allied.

This is the highest trust she'll extend before the party leaves for the city. Allied means she answers questions she's been holding. Including — if asked directly — why she won't go into Night City: *"I had a name in there. I don't anymore."*
