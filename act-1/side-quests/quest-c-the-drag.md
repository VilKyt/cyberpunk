# QUEST C — THE DRAG
*Act 1 Side Quest. Non-Rook. Vehicle race. Tone break.*

---

## GM Background

> *For the GM.*

The Drag runs when enough people show up. No fixed schedule — word goes out through Badlands channels a few days before, a location is named, people come. The location rotates: old industrial sites, decommissioned highway sections, once a year the long straight of the Petrochem access road when the contractor shifts change and the patrol window opens.

This week's venue: the old **Kovacs Freight Junction**, a decommissioned cargo hub about forty minutes south of Rook's camp. Two parallel lines of collapsed warehouse structures create a natural track. One hairpin. One section under a roof that's partially standing — drivers go dark inside it. One long straight where people have been known to hit 180.

Nobody runs this. Nobody profits from it except the winner. People come because the Badlands are very large and very quiet and sometimes you want to go fast.

**This quest has no main-story stakes.** It is noise, speed, colour, and a brief moment when the campaign is not about ancient evil. It also — quietly, optionally — contains one of the clearest illustrations of what the Director's network does to ordinary people.

---

## Getting There

The Drag comes up naturally — one of Rook's camp members mentions it at dinner. Or the party picks it up on the local frequencies Mote monitors for camp security. Or Vera mentions it with a studied lack of interest that means she's been to several.

Nobody stops them going. Rook will lend a vehicle if trust is at Neutral or above, for **20% of the winnings** if they place, nothing if they don't. She asks no other questions about it.

---

## The Venue

The Kovacs Junction: dust, generator-powered lights strung between the warehouse walls, maybe forty people gathered at the starting line. Vehicles parked in rough categories — bikes on the left, cars and trucks on the right. Someone has built a raised viewing stand out of stacked cargo containers.

Atmosphere: loud, warm, smells of fuel and food. There's a vendor with real charcoal-grilled meat. Someone has a speaker system running music too loud for the space. People are making bets with a woman named **Prax** who runs the book out of a folding table and has seen everything and is never surprised by anything.

---

## The Bracket

Eight vehicles. Three rounds. Winner takes the pool.

| Driver | Vehicle | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Dio** (Dio Kestrel) | Modified coupe, midnight blue | Three-time winner. New cyberware. |
| **Gravel** | Flat-nose truck, overengineered | Old Badlands runner, been coming here for twelve years |
| **Suki** | Stripped bike, no plates | Quiet, methodical. Never talks before a race. |
| **The Twins** | Matching bikes, family team | Loud, quick, crash into each other occasionally |
| **Party vehicle** | Whatever Rook lent them | Depends on maintenance state |
| **Two fill-ins** | Various | Background, knocked out round 1 |

**Prax's odds on Dio:** 2:1. He wins, she pays less. She's hoping he doesn't.

---

## Dio

He's twenty-four. He looks good — clear eyes, good reflexes, confident without being loud about it. He nods at whoever's entering the party's vehicle, not unfriendly.

**The cyberware:** New sandivistan (reflex augmentation). He got it three weeks ago from a clinic in Watson. It cost more than he had. He doesn't say how he paid for it.

**What magic-sensitive characters see:** The trace corruption, faint but present. The same Arasaka-brand signature Mote described — the extra layer in the spec sheet that nobody can account for. In Dio it's shallow still, recent install, the integration just beginning. His judgment is slightly degraded already — visible in the way he takes risks that don't quite calculate, not reckless exactly, more like the weighting is slightly off.

He doesn't know. He knows the cyberware works. He knows he's winning.

---

## The Race Mechanic

Use contested checks. Three rounds. Each round is one check.

**The check:** Dexterity (Vehicle Handling). DC to place depends on competition:
- Round 1: DC 13 (against fill-ins and warm-up)
- Round 2 (semifinal): DC 15 (against Gravel or Suki)
- Round 3 (final): DC 17 (against Dio, modified by his cyberware)

**Track hazards — one per round, GM's choice:**

| Hazard | Effect |
|---|---|
| **The hairpin** | DC 13 Dexterity or lose 1 position |
| **The dark section** | Perception DC 12 or late reaction to an obstacle — disadvantage on the round's check |
| **The long straight** | Can push the vehicle — bonus to this round's check, but fail a follow-up DC 11 Dexterity or take vehicle damage |
| **Debris field** | Concentration check (DC 10) or disadvantage — anything distracting the driver |

**Placing:**
- 1st place: Takes the pool minus Prax's cut (approx. 600 eddies if standard entry)
- 2nd: Gets their entry fee back
- 3rd or lower: Entry fee gone (100 eddies standard)

**Non-racers:** Can bet with Prax, watch from the cargo container stands, or work the crowd. People talk at the Drag — it's the one place in the fringe where strangers are welcome for an hour. Information is available here.

---

## Dio in Round 3

Whether the party races against him or not, Dio is in the final. And in the final — under the partial roof, going dark — something is slightly wrong.

He doesn't crash. He wins, or very nearly wins. But in the dark section he takes the racing line a fraction tighter than physics recommends, and the near-miss with the warehouse wall is close enough that people in the stands go quiet for a second.

After the race he's fine. Exhilarated. Doesn't notice anything happened.

**If the party talks to him afterward:**

Dia is friendly, not guarded. He'll tell them about the cyberware — Watson clinic, didn't ask many questions, payment plan he's still not sure about the terms of. He's been winning since. He's saving to get his sister a transit chip so she can get work in the city. He's a good person doing something that is going to hurt him.

> *"You thinking about getting work done? Watson clinic's the one, if you don't want Arasaka pricing. Ask for the night intake, they don't ID."*

This is useful information for Scene 3 (Ghost IDs) if the party hasn't done that yet. And it's a thread: the night intake clinic, the payment plan with unclear terms — Arasaka financing cyberware that carries their integration layer, extended into the fringe population.

**What the party can do for him:** Nothing, immediately. There's no quest here for Dio. He's fine today. The corruption is shallow. But they know his name, and they know the Watson clinic, and they know what they saw in his eyes.

If Mote is told about Dio (and the cyberware detail), he'll want to check the installation. He has opinions about that clinic.

---

## Prax

The book-runner. She's been doing this for seven years. She knows everyone who comes and she has opinions about all of them. If the party wants to gather information about the Badlands fringe — other camps, routes, unusual recent activity, who's been moving where — Prax is the person. She trades in it the way she trades in bets: everything has a value.

Useful intel she'll share for small payment (50–100 eddies) or a winning bet:
- Militech contractor sweeps have doubled in the last month, specifically around the Quiet Mile corridor
- A dead-zone signal southeast of here has been getting stronger. *"Used to come in once a week. Now it's almost daily."*
- Someone bought a significant amount of ghost-ID supplies from a fringe vendor three weeks ago. She doesn't know who. She knows the vendor: he's Splice's neighbour.

---

## Aftermath

No Rook debrief. She doesn't ask how it went. If the party won money, she asks for her cut, pockets it, and says:

> *"Vera could have told you the vehicle pulls left in the hairpin."*

She knew. She didn't say.
