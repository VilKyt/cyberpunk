# QUEST D — THE DEAD ZONE
*Act 1 Side Quest. Minor. Tracking the woman in the pocket dimension.*

---

## GM Background

> *For the GM.*

She has been in the pocket dimension for a very long time. Long enough that she has structured her existence around not being found — by the Director, by his network, by anyone who might carry a trace of the drain and lead it to her threshold.

The dead zone exists because she exists. The pocket dimension she constructed folds against the real world at a fixed point in the Badlands southeast of Rook's camp — a place she chose specifically because the ambient signal density is low there, the corp infrastructure sparse, and the natural Badlands terrain disrupts drone sweeps. The fold is invisible from the outside. What's visible is its effect: within a rough 30-meter radius, the ambient soul-drain trace — present everywhere else in Night City's sphere of influence — is simply absent.

She knows when the party arrives at the threshold. She has known they were coming since the Quiet Mile event — the discharge pulse was visible from inside her dimension, and clean-soul signatures moving through the Badlands are not something she misses. She is not ready to receive them. She is also — and this matters — not quite as alone inside her dimension as she was before she knew they existed.

**Access condition:** The party needs Rook's trust at Allied level (Quest B completed) to get the full grandmother's journal excerpt pointing to *"where the signal stops."* Without it, they might find the dead zone through the Archivist (Quest E) or through Prax's information (Quest C), but they'll have less context for what it means.

---

## Getting There

The journal excerpt Rook shares describes the location in terms of landmarks — specific rock formations, a rusted water tower visible from the east, the way the shadow falls at midday. Survival (Nature) DC 13 to navigate from the description. On a failure, they can still find it but it takes significantly longer — dusk instead of afternoon.

The drive is quiet. The Badlands southeast of camp are emptier than the Quiet Mile corridor — fewer drone routes, older terrain. About forty minutes out, the ambient corruption trace (visible to magic users) begins to thin. Not sharply. Gradually, the way sound fades in a larger room.

---

## Arriving at the Edge

They know they're close when the thinning becomes an absence.

**For magic-sensitive characters:** The purple-black iridescence, present as background noise in every direction since Scene 1, simply stops at a boundary roughly 30 meters across. Inside that circle the world looks different — not brighter, not darker, but *cleaner*. The ground itself seems to breathe more easily. Standing at the edge and looking in is like seeing a patch of the world as it existed before whatever the Director did to it.

**For non-magic characters:** The area is quiet in a way that takes a moment to identify. Wind still moves. Dust still sits. But the hum they've been half-hearing since the tomb — subvocalised, below conscious notice, the frequency of the drain network — is gone. The silence has texture.

---

## The Threshold

At the centre of the dead zone: nothing visible. No structure, no door, no mark on the ground.

But there is a resistance. Not physical. More like the feeling of pressing against something that has no give — not because it's hard but because it is *complete*. Something shaped like a boundary that has been maintained for a very long time.

**Investigation DC 15** or **Arcana DC 12** to understand what it is: a ward. A dimensional fold, held shut from the inside. Not built to be found — built to be unfindable. The fact that they can sense it at all suggests it was designed to *allow* sensing by specific types of people. It is not trying to hide from them. It is simply not open.

---

## She Doesn't Come Out

The ward holds. Nothing happens for a long moment.

Then a crow lands on a rock at the edge of the dead zone. This is not unusual. The crow sits. The crow watches whoever is closest to the threshold with sustained, directed attention that is not crow attention. It cocks its head.

**Insight DC 12:** The bird is being directed. Not possessed — nothing hostile, nothing corrupted. It is being used as an observation point by something on the other side of the threshold. Watched through it, the way a person watches through a window.

The crow does not communicate. It watches for about ninety seconds and then flies off in the direction of the dead zone's centre — which means directly through where the threshold is, disappearing.

---

## The Response

If a magic user approaches the threshold and reaches toward it — hand extended, concentration, not trying to force it but *asking* — the following happens:

A brief warmth in the chest. Not heat. More like the sun returning after a cloud. A sensation of being seen clearly, without judgment, from a distance.

Then: nothing. The threshold doesn't open. But the quality of the ward changes for just a moment — there is a pause in it, a held breath, before it settles back to what it was.

**What it means:** She knows they are clean. She recognises what they are. She is not ready. But she is no longer indifferent.

---

## What the Party Can Learn Here

They can attempt to communicate through the threshold. She won't respond with language. But a magic user who asks specific yes/no questions and attends carefully to the ward's responses (Arcana DC 14) can get:

- *"Are you there?"* — The warmth again. Yes.
- *"Are you safe?"* — A pause. Longer. Then the warmth, qualified — something less clean about it. Mostly yes.
- *"Can we come in?"* — The warmth withdraws. Not hostile withdrawal. Not yet.
- *"Will you let us in eventually?"* — The longest pause. Then: the warmth, definite. Yes. But not now.

They cannot learn her name, her history, or what she knows. That is Act 2 work. What they can learn is that the most important person they'll meet is real, aware of them, and waiting for the right moment.

---

## The Leave-Behind

When the party turns to go, there's something at the base of the threshold marker — a stone, nothing unusual, that wasn't there when they arrived. On the underside: scratched into the rock with something fine, barely visible, three characters in a script that is not any language currently in use in Night City.

**Arcana DC 10** for a magic user: it's Elvish. Old Elvish, archaic form.

It translates, roughly, as: **"I see you."**

She has been alone for a very long time. This is the first thing she has communicated directly to anyone in centuries. It took her the length of their visit to decide to do it.
