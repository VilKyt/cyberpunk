# SCENE 1 — THE TOMB OPENS
*Act 1, Scene 1*

---

## GM Background — The Lich's Logic

> *This is purely for the GM. Players won't know this yet — but it should colour every detail you describe.*

The lich — referred to in Arasaka records as **the Director** — sealed this tomb roughly **a thousand years ago**. It was not a prison built for the party specifically. It was a **decommissioned soul-drain lab**. He had moved on to better methods. The party were residual material — left in a slow bleed array that would consume their life force over centuries, feeding passively back into his phylactery network. Efficient. Forgotten.

The nomads cracking the seal did two things he didn't account for:
- It **interrupted the drain cycle** mid-process — which is why the party is alive but not fully restored. They come back wrong. Sluggish. Like a phone at 4%.
- It **triggered an alert** on a system so old that the flag took three minutes to route through Arasaka's network before anyone noticed what it was.

He is not afraid. He is mildly inconvenienced.

His standing protocol for magic users: **enslave, extract, or if neither is possible, destroy the soul entirely** so it cannot be used against him. The party stumbling out of his old lab is the first genuine threat to the Soulkiller project in centuries. He doesn't fully register it yet.

**The Soulkiller** is his masterwork — a thousand years of refining the phylactery concept into something that runs on corporate infrastructure. Secure Your Soul is the public face. The reality is a continental soul-harvesting engine with Arasaka's server architecture as its nervous system.

---

## Beat 1 — Waking Up Wrong

They don't gasp awake. It's slower and worse than that.

Consciousness returns **in the wrong order** — sensation before thought, sound before sight. Stone beneath them. Cold that has been cold for so long it stopped feeling like temperature and became a property of the universe. The sound of something cracking above. Dust falling.

### Physical State (Mechanical Notes)
- Spell slots and abilities are technically intact but accessing them feels like lifting with a numb arm — there's a half-second of *resistance* before anything fires. This fades over the session as they recover. For the first hour they are not at full power.
- Anyone with passive Perception 14+ notices immediately: the arcane symbols carved into the walls are almost completely drained, the stone itself slightly concave where they were — as if something ate the magic out of them over a very long time.
- The air tastes of **ozone and something organic**. Not rot exactly. More like the smell of a living thing that was here and is now very much gone.

### The Corruption Detail (For Magic Users)
A character who examines the drained symbols closely, or who casts anything in the chamber, notices the residue. Whatever was feeding here left traces — a faint **purple-black iridescence** in the cracks of the stone, like oil in water but *wrong*, almost biological.

It doesn't react to them. It's inert. Ancient and dried out.

But it's recognisable — it's the same substance they encountered in the lich's presence back in Wildemount, where it drove people mad. Here it's a thousand years old and desiccated. The GM note: **this room was a draining facility. They were the patients.**

### First Questions & Answers
| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| How long were we out? | Unknown. The stonework is ancient. The concrete visible in the ceiling above it is not. |
| Where is the lich? | Gone. The chamber is cold and empty. Whatever held them dissipated when the seal broke. |
| Can we cast? | Yes — with resistance. Establish this early. Their magic functions. |

## Beat 2 — The Nomads

Light floods in from above. Voices. The clank of someone dropping down on a rope with a torch and a very human reaction to finding people alive in a sealed tomb.

**The nomad who drops in first:** young, jumpy, second-hand mismatched cyberware, clan patch on his jacket. He has a shotgun he immediately points at the party. He is terrified and covering it with aggression.

This is where **local player characters** can be introduced — they're either already in the nomad group above, or they dropped in with him. Either way, the moment he radios up *"there are people down here — alive people"* and gets back *"that's impossible, the seal was intact"* is the players' first taste that this situation is already strange.

### What the Nomads Know
- The tomb pinged on a low-frequency salvage scanner for **weeks** before they got brave enough to crack it.
- Locals call this area the **Quiet Mile** — animals avoid it, signals go fuzzy, corp drones reroute around it automatically.
- One of the older nomad members: *"My grandmother said there was a woman who warned people away from this ground. Said something was sleeping here that Arasaka wanted badly."* He won't elaborate. He's superstitious and wants to leave immediately.

### Rook — First Contact
She's up top. Not down here yet. The imprisoned characters hear her voice before they see her — calm, clipped, in charge.

She does not ask *"who are you."*

She asks: **"How long were you down there."**

Not a question about them. A question about the *timeline.* She already knows something was sealed here. The discharge confirmed a theory. The players are unexpected but to her, not impossible.

**She saw the discharge** when the seal broke — a pulse of purple-black light that shot straight up and dissipated. She hasn't told anyone. She's filing it.

---

## Beat 3 — First Contact With the World

Before Arasaka arrives, give the players **one short window** — maybe five minutes of table time — to react to their environment.

### Key Sensory Moments to Offer
- A player catches a glimpse of Rook's **Quadra** parked nearby — a battered vehicle with a cracked windshield and running lights. To anyone from Wildemount: a horseless metal carriage that growls.
- Someone sees Rook with a **phone** out. A glowing rectangle. She's talking to it and it talks back.
- The **sky**: smoggy, orange at the horizon, a distant cluster of towers lit like embers. Night City on the skyline. None of them have words for it.

### The Magic Reaction
If any player casts visibly — even a cantrip, even Mage Hand — have one of the younger nomads **go completely still**. Not attack. Just back against the wall, eyes wide, very quiet.

That reaction tells the imprisoned characters everything about how magic works in this world: it doesn't. Except it just did.

## Beat 4 — Arasaka Responds

The seal broke hard. Old stonework cracking, a faint arcane discharge that any spellcaster felt — and something registered in Arasaka's network. The alert was old, the routing slow. But the response is fast.

**Timeline:** approximately **8 minutes** from seal-break to rotors. Beats 1–3 happen in this window. Make it feel slightly rushed without telegraphing why.

### The Agents
A single **AV** drops toward the Quiet Mile — black, angular, no markings, running silent until it isn't. Two agents fast-rope down. Plain clothes over light armour. Neural ports visible at the temple. Professional.

**The corruption tell:** when the senior agent steps into the residual discharge still hanging near the tomb entrance, he pauses — a fraction of a second. His eyes catch the light wrong. A faint **purple tint at the edge of his iris** pulses once and settles. He doesn't notice. His partner doesn't notice. Any magic-sensitive player character will.

This is the corruption, refined. Not the raving flesh-warping madness from Wildemount — **a thousand years of improvement**. These agents are *branded*. The soul-drain network runs through them faintly. They're loyal, effective, and don't know why at a level below conscious thought. They'd pass a psych evaluation.

### The Netrunner's Scan
The second agent — the netrunner — scans the players. Her eyes go briefly white. She looks at her results. She does not read them aloud.

What she's seeing: soul-integrity readings so clean and untampered they flag as **errors**. Every human in Night City carries trace levels of the soul-drain. The players don't. To her scanner, they barely register as people. She files it as equipment malfunction and calls for collection.

### Escalation Table (Player-Driven)

| Player Action | Where It Goes |
|---|---|
| Aggression | Agents call for backup — more AVs incoming, clock starts |
| Run with the nomads | Rook makes the call — chase scene, first taste of Night City roads |
| Talk / bluff | Netrunner is confused by her scans, curious enough to stall — not enough to disobey |
| Visible magic | Immediate priority extraction order — the Director notified directly |
| **Divine magic targeting the corruption** | The branded agent *seizes* — purple pulses, his partner backs away. Neither side knows what happened. Everything escalates. |

### The Radio Call
However the scene resolves, one of the agents gets a transmission out before or during the chaos:

> *"Confirmed. Convergence file — live subjects, unprocessed. Notify the Director. Souls are clean."*

**Souls are clean.**

The players don't know what that means yet. Rook, if she hears it — and she does — goes very still.
