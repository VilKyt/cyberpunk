# NOTORIETY
*Two independent tracks. Five tiers each. They escalate separately and interact.*

---

## Overview

The party has two notoriety counters — one with **Arasaka**, one with **Militech**. They start at zero. They climb independently. A party that antagonises both corporations simultaneously faces responses from two directions, occasionally getting in each other's way, but never safe.

Notoriety does not decay passively. It requires active effort to reduce.

**The core asymmetry:**
- Arasaka wants the party **captured or consumed** — clean souls are the calibration source Soulkiller needs. At high tiers, they deploy magic. They know exactly what the party is.
- Militech wants the party **captured and studied** — they don't know about magic, but they know Arasaka has an unexplained edge, and these people seem to be part of it. At high tiers they theorise. They never quite figure it out.

## Arasaka Notoriety

### Tier 1 — Signal Noise
*Triggered by: checkpoint flag (Scene 4), visible use of unusual ability near corp infrastructure, any CVGNC-OPS ping logged.*

The party exists as a data anomaly. No active pursuit. Automated systems flag them; no human has reviewed the flags yet.

**Response:**
- Drone passes increase in areas the party has been seen
- Automated soul-integrity scanners log anomalous readings if the party moves through monitored zones
- Ghost IDs continue to work — the flag is on the soul-reading layer, not the ID layer

**GM note:** The party probably doesn't know they have Tier 1. It's background. It becomes relevant when they do something else and discover the system was already watching.

---

### Tier 2 — Person of Interest
*Triggered by: engaging Arasaka contractors in combat, repeated CVGNC-OPS pings, Convergence flag confirmed by a human duty officer.*

A human analyst has now reviewed the anomaly flags. The party has been assigned a file. The CVGNC-OPS chain is aware of them as a specific target, not just background noise.

**Response:**
- Plainclothes investigators in areas the party frequents — not combatants, surveillance only
- One response team on standby: two agents (as per Scene 1 — branded, professional, light armour)
- The agents will attempt observation before engagement; they want to confirm the party before calling for extraction

**The corruption tell:** Any magic-sensitive character who spots a Tier 2 surveillance operative will see the familiar purple-black iridescence — trace, barely visible. These are branded agents. Loyal below conscious thought. They don't know what they're looking for, only that their orders feel natural and urgent.

---

### Tier 3 — Priority Target
*Triggered by: killing Arasaka agents, visible arcane magic use on monitored infrastructure, multiple Tier 2 escalation events.*

The Convergence file is fully active. A senior CVGNC-OPS officer has been assigned. More importantly: the Director has been briefed. He is not personally engaged yet — but he has directed specific assets to this matter.

**Response:**
- Tactical response teams: four agents, coordinated, proper military gear, neural-linked comms
- **First appearance of the Hounds** — Arasaka's anomalous-threat unit. Small team (2–3 operatives). They do not announce what they can do.

**The Hounds:**
These operatives have been given limited arcane capability through partial phylactery integration — a deliberate, controlled version of the corruption process. The Director developed this specifically for handling magic-capable threats. They are not full magic users. They have:
- *Soul-sense:* passive ability to detect magic users within 30 ft without any check. They cannot be surprised by spellcasting.
- *Suppression field:* once per encounter, one Hound can activate a suppression pulse (action). All spellcasters within 20 ft must succeed on a DC 14 Concentration check or their next spell fails. The pulse is visible to magic-sensitive characters as a brief orange-gold flare — the opposite colour to the purple-black corruption.
- *Branded resilience:* advantage on saves against magical effects. The soul-drain integration partially insulates them.

**What the party notices:** The Hounds fight differently. They position to counter spellcasters specifically. They react to casting before the spell is visible to normal eyes. A Perception DC 14 catches the micro-tells — they flinch slightly when a spell is prepared, as if hearing a sound no one else can hear.

**Capture intent begins here.** The Hounds carry restraint hardware — neural suppressors (designed to interfere with cyberware; affects magic interfaces by accident rather than design). They would prefer live extraction. They will kill if necessary.

---

### Tier 4 — Convergence Protocol Active
*Triggered by: neutralising a Hound team, destroying Arasaka infrastructure connected to Soulkiller, repeated escape from Tier 3 response.*

The Director is personally engaged. He has assigned Convergence extraction resources — assets he does not deploy for ordinary corporate problems.

**Response:**
- Full Convergence team: six tactical operatives + two Channelers
- Hounds on standby as backup
- Active warrant in Arasaka's Night City system — all Arasaka-affiliated NPCs will report party sightings

**The Channelers:**
These are the ones that should disturb the party. They are magic users — real ones, captured and soul-drained to the point where the obedience programming runs underneath everything. They cast. They cannot be reasoned with. Charisma checks do not work. Persuasion, Deception, Intimidation — nothing registers. There is no person present to appeal to.

They operate on orders and targeting data and nothing else.

*This is the doll mechanic.* See `ideas.md`.

**Mechanically:**
- Immune to Charisma-based effects
- Cast from a limited list (the Director has given them specific tools, not freedom): *Hold Person, Silence, Dispel Magic, Counterspell, Blindness/Deafness*
- Can be interrupted via **Override quickhack** (2 RAM) — this breaks the obedience signal for 1 round, making them neutral and non-functional. They don't attack their handlers. They simply stop.
- On a Perception DC 16 or Arcana DC 12: a magic-sensitive character can see that the Channelers' eyes are wrong — not the subtle iridescence of branded agents, but a steady, deep purple-black that has replaced most of the natural colour. They are almost entirely consumed.

**Capture is still preferred.** But at this tier, if extraction isn't possible, the Director authorises soul-extraction in the field — not clean (messy, incomplete), but better than letting clean souls act against him.

---

### Tier 5 — Director's Attention
*Triggered by: killing Channelers, direct interference with Soulkiller infrastructure, capturing or destroying high-level CVGNC-OPS assets.*

The Director has stopped delegating. This is no longer a standard corporate security matter.

**Response:**
- **Adam Smasher** is deployed if the situation requires physical resolution. See NPC file. He cannot be killed conventionally — death magic finds no anchor. He can be disabled, escaped from, or (with significant effort) shut down temporarily. He will return.
- A personal extraction order is active — the Director wants the party alive, but if that's not achievable, he wants their souls destroyed completely so they cannot be used against him
- Arasaka's full Night City apparatus treats the party as the highest active threat

**The Director's personal interest:** If the party reaches Tier 5, the Director may make direct contact — not physical, but through an intermediary or a message. He is curious. He has not encountered genuinely clean souls acting against him in centuries. He will attempt to understand them before destroying them. This is a window, not a safety.

## Militech Notoriety

### Tier 1 — Flagged Civilians
*Triggered by: Arroyo Gate combat, killing Militech-affiliated contractors, significant property damage to Militech assets.*

A bounty is posted in contractor networks. Standard wanted notice — physical descriptions, last known location, vehicle. No corp resources committed yet.

**Response:**
- Fringe contractors and bounty hunters aware of the party
- Militech checkpoints in the corridor have their descriptions
- No active pursuit teams

---

### Tier 2 — Active Bounty
*Triggered by: multiple Militech contractor engagements, destruction of Militech property worth more than 10,000 eddies, escaping a Tier 1 bounty response.*

A corporate intelligence analyst has reviewed the incidents. The party is now a named file, not just a flag. Bounty raised to a level that attracts professional hunters.

**Response:**
- Bounty hunters (professional, better-equipped than contractors): 1–2 per encounter, coordinated with informant networks
- Militech patrols in areas the party frequents have party photos
- Informant payments active — fringe vendors and fixers may sell party location for the right price

---

### Tier 3 — Corporate Persons of Interest
*Triggered by: killing Militech regulars, accessing Militech-secured infrastructure, continued evasion of Tier 2 response.*

A Militech intelligence officer has flagged something. The party's scan results — from Arroyo Gate cameras, from bounty hunter reports — are inconsistent with normal human profiles. They heal faster than they should. Their biometrics don't quite fit. Something is different, and Militech has noticed that Arasaka is *also* looking for them.

That second fact matters more than the first.

**Response:**
- Corporate soldiers: four-person fireteams, proper military gear, coordinated tactics
- An intelligence operative assigned specifically to understand what the party is
- **Capture priority begins here.** Kill orders are downgraded to extraction orders. Whoever these people are, they are worth more alive.

**What Militech theorises:** The intelligence operative files a report that reads: *"Subjects demonstrate anomalous biological cohesion. No cyberware that accounts for observed capability. Arasaka has active Convergence protocol on these subjects — classification unknown. Recommend live capture for study. If these subjects are why Arasaka's loyalty integration outperforms our own, we need to understand the mechanism."*

They are trying to reverse-engineer the soul-drain advantage without knowing the soul-drain exists.

---

### Tier 4 — Extraction Priority
*Triggered by: killing corporate Militech soldiers, destroying significant Militech assets, repeated escape from Tier 3 response.*

High command has been briefed. The working theory: the party is either a Militech black project they weren't told about, or an independent phenomenon that both corps are chasing. Either way, they need to be acquired before Arasaka gets them.

**Response:**
- Extraction specialists: six-person team, advanced gear, neural-linked, equipped for live capture (shock rounds, neural disruptors, hardened restraints)
- Arasaka hunters may attempt to interfere with Militech extraction operations — creating three-way encounters
- Militech intelligence is now tracking party movements through Night City's camera grid

**The offer:** At Tier 4, a Militech officer may attempt direct contact rather than combat — a meeting, through an intermediary, with an offer. *Work for us, or work against Arasaka with our support. We'll drop the bounty. We'll provide resources. We want to understand what you are and we'd prefer to do it cooperatively.*

This offer is genuine and also has a catch: they intend to run extensive testing. They will not drop this line of interest once begun. The cooperation is the trap.

---

### Tier 5 — Corporate War Asset
*Triggered by: killing Militech extraction operatives, rejecting or betraying the Tier 4 offer, destroying Militech Night City infrastructure.*

Militech high command has reclassified the party as a live warfare asset that must be denied to Arasaka. If they cannot be acquired, they must be eliminated — not because of the bounty, but because Arasaka having them is worse than no one having them.

**Response:**
- Military-grade response: AV teams, coordinated street-level and aerial pursuit
- Militech Night City assets fully mobilised
- Arasaka will be aware of the Militech escalation and may attempt to intercept — creating genuine three-faction combat scenarios

## Cross-Faction Dynamics

### High Arasaka, Low Militech
Militech notices that Arasaka is very interested in these people. They become curious before they become hostile. A party in this position may receive an approach from Militech intelligence before any combat — *"We know Arasaka is after you. So do we. Let's talk."*

### High Militech, Low Arasaka
The party has been disrupting Militech operations without drawing significant Arasaka attention. This is sustainable for a while. Militech's lack of magical capability means their responses, however well-resourced, don't threaten the party the way Arasaka's do.

### High Both (Tier 3+/Tier 3+)
Both corporations are running operations in the same city. At this level, their response teams will occasionally encounter each other while pursuing the party — creating situations where the party can exploit the conflict, slip away while the corps engage, or be caught between two directions at once.

Neither corporation will cooperate with the other to catch the party. Their competitive relationship runs too deep. This is the only silver lining.

### The Militech-Arasaka Intel Gap
Militech suspects Arasaka has an unexplained operational advantage. They believe the party is connected to that advantage. They do not know about magic, souls, or the Director. If the party ever gives Militech the information they need — even partially — Militech becomes a much more dangerous and motivated actor. They don't need to understand magic fully to use the party's intelligence against Arasaka's infrastructure.

## Reducing Notoriety

Notoriety does not expire. It must be actively addressed.

| Method | Reduction | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Lay low** — avoid all faction contact | −1 tier after one full in-game week of no incidents | Slow. Requires genuine inactivity. |
| **Ghost run** — netrunner scrubs relevant records | −1 tier on success | Requires access to the faction's relevant database. Difficult. Leaves traces if done imperfectly. |
| **Complete a faction job** | −1 tier (Militech only at tiers 1–2) | Militech will offer work to reduce bounties. Arasaka does not negotiate with convergence targets. |
| **Bribe / pay off** | −1 tier (Militech only, tiers 1–2) | Arasaka's notoriety system is not bounty-based — money doesn't move it. |
| **Fake death** | −2 tiers if convincing | Requires a body (or a very good facsimile), forged death record, and staying out of cameras for an extended period. Splice or a Night City fixer can help arrange this. |
| **Major faction damage** | Freeze — stops escalation temporarily | Destroying a key CVGNC-OPS node (Arasaka) or Militech regional command (Militech) doesn't reduce notoriety but halts active pursuit while the faction rebuilds its operation. |

**Arasaka notoriety cannot drop below Tier 1** once the Convergence file is active. The Director knows they exist. The best the party can achieve is reducing the active pursuit intensity, not erasing themselves from his attention.
