# WORLD LORE — THE SOULKILLER & THE DIRECTOR'S PLAN

## The Phylactery Problem
A lich's phylactery is a single point of failure.
The Director spent centuries solving this.

## The Idea
Distribute the phylactery concept across a network.
Instead of one object containing his soul fragment —
millions of soul-shard micro-extractions, stored in
corporate server architecture, feeding back continuously.

Every citizen using Arasaka's neural interface products
donates a microscopic soul-integrity fragment per transaction.
Individually meaningless. At continental scale: godlike.

## The Problem He Hasn't Solved Yet
Soulkiller works in theory. In practice, extracted engrams
degrade. Soul-integrity fractures on capture. Every test
subject to date has produced a hollow copy — a recording,
not a soul. The network receives data but not the real thing.

The Director has been working on this for twenty years.
The failure condition: he has nothing clean to calibrate against.
Everyone in Night City has been touched by ambient corruption.
There is nothing pure enough to use as a baseline.

## Present Day — The Transition

The party waking up changes everything.

Silverhand is captured in the same window — the first
extraction that actually holds. His soul-integrity is
degraded from decades of corp exposure, but compared to
every other subject, he is clean enough. His chip works.
It is the first Soulkiller extraction that produces
something real rather than residue.

The party are the next logical step. Souls sealed for a
thousand years, never touched by the network, never
ambient-corrupted. If Silverhand is a proof of concept,
the party are the proof that it scales.

The Director is in transition. He is not maintaining
a continental network — he is on the verge of building one.
The party woke up at the worst possible moment.

## Secure Your Soul (Public)
Arasaka's premium service. Full consciousness backup.
"Live forever." The marketing exists. The infrastructure
to actually do it at scale does not yet. Silverhand's chip
is what changes that. His extraction is the blueprint.

## Soulkiller (Reality)
The extraction engine beneath Secure Your Soul.
Twenty years in development. Finally working, as of now.
Can be weaponised — targeted soul destruction, leaving
a body alive but hollow. The Director has used cruder
versions of this for years. The refined version is new.

## The Corruption
Visible to magic users as purple-black iridescence.
This predates Soulkiller — it's the residue of older methods.
- Wildemount (1,000 years ago): raw, unstable, drove victims mad
- Night City (present): refined, controlled, used as branding
- Arasaka senior staff are marked — they don't know
- Undercity population shows elevated corruption — collateral
  from living near infrastructure running old extraction tech

Soulkiller, when fully deployed, will produce something
different from corruption. Cleaner. More complete. Worse.

## Why the Party Matters
Their souls are clean — never touched by the network.
They can perceive the corruption. They can disrupt it.
They are the calibration source the Director has been
looking for. And they escaped before he could use them.

If he captures them and runs the extraction, Soulkiller
goes from proof-of-concept to continental deployment.
If they stop him, it stays a prototype.

This is the window.
