# WORLD TIMELINE

## Ancient Era (Wildemount)
- Party active in Wildemount / Exandria
- Encounter the lich in his sanctum
- Defeated, sealed in soul-drain tomb

## The Long Gap (~1,000 years)
- Lich continues refining soul-drain technology
- Relocates to what will become Night City region
- Identity changes every few decades
- Begins corporate infiltration ~200 years ago
- Founds / takes over Arasaka ~80 years ago
- Old methods: branding, corruption, partial soul-drain through cyberware
- Soulkiller research begins ~20 years ago — still experimental
- Previous attempts failed: engrams extracted but unstable, soul-integrity degraded on capture
- The problem: no sufficiently clean source material to calibrate against

## Present Day — 2077 (Acts 1–3)
- Nomads crack the tomb seal
- Party wakes — first clean souls on the continent in centuries
- Director issues Convergence File collection order
- Soulkiller already operational: Silverhand's engram is the proof-of-concept, running in V's head
- Clock: the Director is moving toward full deployment — the Tower

## Act 2 — Night City
- Watson: the Artist, Judy, the tunnels, the Archivist (fragmented)
- The three nodes; full Archivist restoration; recognition scene
- Misty's reading; Rogue; V and Silverhand
- The Arasaka Tower Raid — party's angle, alongside V's
  - Adam Smasher; the Director; the extraction mechanism
  - Party outcome TBD in play

## The Tower — Soulkiller Full Deployment
- The Director reaches the threshold: Soulkiller goes continental
- Every Arasaka neural interface user, every Secure Your Soul subscriber,
  every person touched by the ambient drain — harvested simultaneously
- Night City is the test case. If it works here, it scales globally.
- Mass death or mass enslavement at scale
- This is what Misty's hidden card means. This is what cannot be stopped
  from within the present — only witnessed, or escaped, or outlasted.

## Act 3/4 — The Far Future (stub)
- The Archivist proposes temporal displacement: a one-way translation forward
- His dunamancy — time, gravity, potential — is the mechanism
- The destination: far future. Spaceships. A world shaped by what happened here.
- What the party finds there, and whether it gives them a way back to change
  anything, is the culmination of the campaign.
- *Build this out when the shape of Acts 2–3 is clearer.*
