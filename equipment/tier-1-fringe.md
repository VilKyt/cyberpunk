## Cyberware

### C-Link — Neural Interface (Base)
*Subdermal processor with antenna cluster. The foundation layer. Nothing else works without it.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Install** | Mote (base hardware). 20 min, local anaesthetic. |
| **Cost** | 400 €$ |

**What it enables:**
- Net runs and program use (cyberdeck required separately)
- Quickhacks via RAM (cyberdeck required)
- Smart weapon targeting (optics with Smart Link also required)
- AR overlay reception — ads, navigation, comms

**Passive functions (always on once installed):**
- Chip ID broadcast — the party's ghost IDs transmit through this
- Encrypted voice/text calls
- Eddy wallet — contactless payment, balance stored on chip
- ID document storage

**Passive risk in Night City:**
Neural ad networks push signals through the C-Link without consent. On first entry into a dense commercial area — and occasionally thereafter — the GM may call for a **Wisdom save (DC varies: DC 10 for street-level spam, DC 14 for targeted corpo content)**. On a failure: disadvantage on Perception and Insight checks for 1 hour, as the brain chases a synthetic dopamine trace instead of paying attention.

*The anomaly:* The party's chips seated differently from standard during Splice's installation. Their clean-soul baseline means the ad-targeting network reads them as anomalous and may serve unfiltered psych-ops content rather than standard commercial targeting. First exposure in the city may be disorienting in ways that go beyond a normal citizen's experience. GM discretion on severity.

### Smart Weapons
*Requires: C-Link + Optics with Smart Weapon Link. Without both, the weapon will not fire.*

Smart weapons **auto-hit** — no attack roll. The targeting system handles it. Number of shots per round equals the character's number of attacks.

**Tradeoff:** Smart weapons do not double on unarmored targets. Consistent output, not spectacular. They are expensive and rare at Tier 1 — Vera might have one, fringe vendors rarely do.

| Weapon | Damage | Notes | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Smart Pistol** | 1d4 per shot | Light, range 30/90 ft | 2,000 €$ |
| **Smart SMG** | 1d6 per shot | Two-handed, range 30/90 ft | 3,500 €$ |
| **Smart Rifle** | 1d8 per shot | Two-handed, range 60/180 ft. More naturally Tier 2. | 5,000 €$ |

*Ammunition for smart weapons is standard. The intelligence is in the barrel and the chip, not the round.*

### RAM & Quickhacks
*Real-world hacking. No architecture, no node map — direct injection on single targets or devices within range. Costs RAM, not program slots.*

RAM is spent mid-combat or mid-scene, using the C-Link + cyberdeck to reach nearby electronics or cyberware-enhanced targets. **Range: 30 ft** unless stated otherwise.

| Quickhack | RAM Cost | Action | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Distract Device** | 1 | Bonus action | One electronic device within 30 ft malfunctions for 1 round — alarm silenced, camera loops, door lock cycles, etc. |
| **Reboot Optics** | 1 | Action | Target with cybernetic eyes: **blinded** until end of their next turn. CON save DC 12 negates. |
| **Short Circuit** | 1 | Action | 1d6 lightning damage to one cyberware-enhanced target. No save. Ineffective against non-augmented targets. |
| **Contagion** | 2 | Action | 2d6 lightning damage to one cyberware-enhanced target. If the target has cyberware, one adjacent cyberware-enhanced creature also takes 1d6 lightning damage automatically. |
| **Override** | 2 | Action | *Against obedience-programmed targets only (dolls, deeply branded Arasaka staff).* Interrupts programming for 1 round — target becomes neutral, stops attacking, stops following orders. **Charisma checks do not work on these targets. This does.** |

**RAM recovery:** All spent RAM returns on a short rest.

*Note: Higher-tier cyberdecks provide more RAM (6, 8) and additional quickhack options. The Override quickhack remains rare — its existence is known to Voodoo Boys and a handful of fringe netrunners, but not widely.*
