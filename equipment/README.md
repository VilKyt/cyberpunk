## Currency

**Eddies (€$)** are the standard unit. Approximate value context:
- A decent meal in the fringe: 10–20 €$
- A night's lodging, fringe: 30–60 €$
- A bribe to a tired checkpoint guard: 300 €$
- A Tier 1 weapon, used condition: 200–500 €$
- A basic cyberdeck: 800–1,200 €$
- Smart weapon (Tier 1): 2,000+ €$

Gold and coinage from Wildemount have melt value only. Henk at the waystation will assess and pay accordingly — roughly 5–10 €$ per gram of gold equivalent, depending on purity.

## Firearms vs. Armor

This rule applies to all ranged weapons that use ballistic ammunition.

**Double the damage dice** when the target is:
- **Unarmored** — AC comes from Dexterity only, no armor equipment
- Wearing **Wildemount magic armor** — enchanted for swords and spells; the enchantment provides no ballistic resistance

**Normal damage** when the target wears:
- Modern light armor (leather jacket, kevlar vest): AC 12–13
- Modern medium armor (tactical vest, combat plating): AC 14–16
- Modern heavy armor (full combat rig, exo-frame): AC 17+

**The reasoning:** A pistol round was designed for tissue resistance, not enchanted steel. Against unarmored flesh, or armor that was never built to stop bullets, the kinetic energy transfers completely. Against a ballistic vest, it's doing exactly what the vest was designed for.

**Smart weapons are exempt.** Their targeting system calibrates for tissue resistance automatically. Smart weapon damage does not double on unarmored targets — consistent output is their design. It's the tradeoff for auto-hit.

## Cyberware — General Notes

- Cyberware requires a **C-Link (neural interface)** as the base layer. Nothing else installs cleanly without it.
- Items marked **(Mote)** are available at Rook's camp. Items marked **(Night City)** require a city ripper doc.
- Installation takes 20–30 minutes per item, requires a short rest before the item functions fully.
- **Arasaka-manufactured cyberware** carries an unspecified integration layer (see Mote's notes). It functions. It is also watching.

## Quickhacks vs. Programs

Two separate systems. Do not confuse them.

| System | When Used | Resource | Refreshes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Quickhacks** | Real world, mid-combat, single targets/devices | RAM | Short rest |
| **Programs** | During a full net run (node architecture) | Program slots | Per run |

A character needs a **cyberdeck** for both. RAM and program slots are listed on the deck.
