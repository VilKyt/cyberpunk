## Dolls (Neuromancer-inspired)

Inspired by the dolls in *Neuromancer* — specifically how Molly was trafficked and abused without awareness. Not a sexual fantasy, but a darker mechanic: some of the lich's staff operate purely on obedience programming, with no conscious awareness of their situation (echoing the "innie/outie" severance in *Severance*).

**Gameplay implication:** Charisma/persuasion checks don't work on these NPCs — they can't be reasoned with, bribed, or charmed because there's no "person" present to appeal to. However, quickhack-type interruptions (signal disruption, daemon injection) could break through by interfering with the programming itself.

**Narrative potential:** Unsettling discovery moment when players realize the guards aren't ignoring them — they literally cannot process the interaction.

## Vampires (Blade-inspired)

A callback to the opening of *Blade* — since we already have a lich, a small number of very well-hidden vampires could exist as his generals or independent power players. Possible *World of Darkness* RPG flavor.

**The hook:** The players need something from a vampire (information, an object, a favor). This leads them to a club — surface-level a high-end nightclub, underneath it a party where the living are the entertainment/food. The players enter not knowing, or knowing and going anyway.

**Constraints to keep it grounded:**
- Vampires are *extremely* rare and hidden — not a faction, more like an open secret among the city's elite.
- Their existence shouldn't overshadow the lich as the primary supernatural threat; they're adjacent, not competing.
- The "vampires as generals" angle could explain how the lich maintains control without showing his hand directly.
