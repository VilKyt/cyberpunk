## GM Background

> *For the GM only.*

Vampires exist in Night City. Not many. Not openly. They have learned — over centuries, in some cases — that visibility is extinction. In Wildemount they were a known threat, hunted and catalogued. In Night City they are not classified as anything, because no one who has encountered them has survived to file a report and had it believed.

**Club Blood** is the closest thing they have to a social institution. The surface level is a real nightclub — exclusive, expensive, genuinely popular among the kind of people who have enough money that their names don't appear in gossip columns. It has rotating front names and shell ownership structures. Anyone looking for it in the city's business registry will find nothing. The address circulates by word of mouth, which means it doesn't circulate at all unless you're already inside the network.

Below the main floor: a second space. Access is through a door that looks like a utility corridor. The lower level is where the actual gathering happens — invitation only, small numbers, and the living who attend are there by choice. Some know exactly what they're attending. Others are there for reasons they don't fully understand and will not remember clearly afterward.

No hunting on the premises. That rule is enforced. What members do outside the club is not Club Blood's business.

**The soul-drain problem:** the ambient Soulkiller signal that pervades Night City doesn't kill vampires — they are not mortal in the standard sense — but it degrades something. The older the vampire's lineage, the more resistant they are. The newer ones, the ones turned in this city without proper grounding, are more vulnerable. The three spawn found in the Watson tunnels are the result: they held their membership for years, attended, were functional enough to participate in the life of the club. Then the signal took what it takes, and they became what they became, and no one came looking for them. The club wrote them off. The club does not go looking for what it loses.

The older members know this is a problem they haven't solved.

## The Arrangement With the Director

The Director is aware Club Blood exists. He has been aware since before Night City was Night City — some of the oldest members predate the city entirely, and he knew them in Wildemount as the dangerous things they were.

He does not hunt them. They do not cross him. What they have, functionally, is a mutual non-aggression pact with services attached.

**What the Director gets:** selective removal. When the Director needs someone to disappear — not killed cleanly, not in a way that creates a Militech or NCPD investigation, but *gone*, consumed, no trace — he routes the name through a contact. The club's older members take care of it. They have been doing this for decades. To them it is simply hunting with better targeting.

**What the club gets:** absence. Arasaka's surveillance grid doesn't notice them. Their shell companies don't get audited. The city's facial recognition infrastructure has a blind spot around their premises. These aren't gifts the Director gives them out of goodwill. They are ongoing maintenance that he performs because it costs him very little and keeps useful tools in the city.

It is not a warm arrangement. The Director finds them beneath his interest most of the time. The club's older members know they exist at his tolerance and shape their behaviour accordingly.

> *Neither party discusses this openly. It is the kind of arrangement that works precisely because no one involved wants to examine it too closely.*

## The Older Members — Wildemount Remnants

Two or three of Club Blood's current members are genuinely ancient. Not Night City old. Not Night City's kind of old.

They arrived over centuries — displaced, following the lich's migration route the way that certain predators follow prey. In Wildemount they operated in the open, more or less, because the world had a framework for what they were. In this world they have had to adapt: learn the technology, learn the social structures, learn to pass. They have had a thousand years to practice.

They know what the Director is. They knew him before he was what he is now. They do not discuss this with the younger members of the club. The Director's arrangement with them predates Night City, predates Arasaka, predates most things that currently exist. Some of the services they've rendered were in Wildemount.

**What they know:**
- The Director's original form, his name before the transformation, his methods in the old world
- The soul-drain in broad terms — they have watched its effects for centuries
- The party, potentially, by reputation from Wildemount. Whether they will acknowledge this is another matter.

**What they want:** to continue existing. They have been surviving for a very long time. They are very good at it. The party's appearance — souls that read as clean, Wildemount-origin — is something they will notice immediately and find profoundly interesting. They have not encountered anything like this in over a thousand years.

This does not mean they are allies. It means they are paying attention.

## The Surface Club — Running It

The front-of-house operates as a genuine nightclub. Staff are human, mostly unaware of what the building contains. The music is exceptional. The drinks are expensive. The aesthetic is controlled excess — black, crimson, brutalist architecture softened by expensive lighting. The kind of place where the city's corporate upper tier comes to perform wealth at each other.

The door policy is real: the surface club is actually difficult to get into. This is part of the cover. Nobody suspicious of an exclusive nightclub — they're everywhere. The exclusivity makes the real operation invisible.

**Getting in (surface level):** money, reputation, or being on someone's list. Night City's elite club access. Standard methods. The party will have access once their notoriety reaches a level where their names mean something.

**Getting below:** requires either an invitation from a member, or the membership chips found on the spawn — which will get the party as far as the access corridor before someone wants to know how they came by them. That conversation will be interesting.

## First Visit — Possible Beats

**The door:** the chips from the tunnels will be recognised. They're old. The face they belong to is not standing in front of this door. Whoever checks them will know this. Whether they let the party through depends on what the party says about where the chips came from.

**The surface floor:** they may spend time here before finding the access point. The clientele is worth observing — the kind of people who move through Night City without friction. One or two of them may notice the party in a way that feels different from normal social interest.

**The lower level:** if they get in, they will understand very quickly that they are not in an ordinary room. The social dynamics are wrong. The way people hold themselves. The age visible in eyes that don't match faces. And the specific feeling — magic-sensitive characters will catch it immediately — of multiple very old predators in close proximity choosing to be civilised.

**The ancient members:** one of them will approach. Formally. The party's soul-readings are unusual enough that this is not optional for someone who can sense what they're sensing. The conversation that follows is up to the table.

## What the Party Can Learn Here

**Freely (if they get in and talk):**
- The Director is real. Ancient. The oldest members use a different name for him — one from before Arasaka, before Night City. They will not say it easily.
- The soul-drain has been running longer than most living things have been alive. They have watched it.
- The spawn found in the tunnels — *those were ours, once. The city took them. The city takes everything, eventually.*

**With significant trust:**
- What the Director was before the transformation. What he wanted. What the soul-drain was originally for.
- The arrangement: they will not describe it in detail, but they will confirm it exists and has existed for a very long time. They are not ashamed of it. They are pragmatic.
- One of the ancient members may have knowledge of the Archivist — not as an AI fragment, but as the wizard he was. They knew him. This can be a thread.

**At their discretion:**
- Names. The Director's original name. The Archivist's name, possibly. Information that changes the shape of everything the party thinks they know.

---

## What the Club Wants From the Party

Nothing. Not yet. They are watching.

The ancient members have been alive long enough to know that interesting things move through the world in clusters. The party is interesting. Whatever brought them here — whatever made their souls clean when the entire city's soul-rot is decades advanced — is more interesting than anything that has happened in Night City in a long time.

They will extend professional courtesy. They will not be rushed. They have all the time in the world, in the most literal sense.

If the party becomes a direct threat to the arrangement, that changes things.
