## First Impression

She doesn't seem surprised to see people Rook sent. She's seen enough of them. She looks the party over with the systematic attention of someone cataloguing, not judging. Her questions are direct and practical. She is interested in the party's anomalous signature in a way that is intellectual before it is suspicious.

The shop behind her is orderly under the apparent chaos — tools exactly where they should be, equipment calibrated, supplies sorted. She is competent and she knows it and she doesn't need anyone else to know it.

---

## Background

She had a different name and a different life eight years ago. She doesn't discuss either. The record she needed deleted from the Watson civil database is the last connection between the person she was and the person she is — a cross-reference in a civil registry that would let someone patient enough link the two.

She changed sides on something. She is not proud of which side she was originally on. She is not going to tell anyone what that side was unless she trusts them considerably more than she trusts most people, which is a bar she has set deliberately high.

What she did with the eight years since: learned medicine, learned cyberware installation, built a practice on the fringe doing work for people who need to not exist or need to start existing. She is good at this. She has a reputation that travels by word of mouth only, which is the best kind.

She has had contact with Voodoo Boys adjacent operators — not membership, not ideology, just overlap. She knows things that are circulating in those circles without knowing what to make of them yet.

---

## What She Offers

### Ghost ID Packages
Fabricated identities: name, citizenship tier, basic financial history, plausible backstory. Good enough for automated scans and most human checkpoints. Not good enough for Arasaka deep-scan. She's honest about this.

### Chip Installation
Clean, fast, professional. Twenty minutes per person. She works in silence except for clinical narration of what she's doing, which patients find either reassuring or deeply unsettling depending on temperament.

### Database Access Brokering
She knows which nodes hold what, which ones have weak points, which ones are under active Arasaka monitoring. She can point people at targets. She doesn't run the jobs herself — she lost her best netrunner contact recently and hasn't replaced them.

### Information
She observes. She logs. She has been watching the fringe for eight years and has a very good sense of what patterns mean. If the party needs to know something about Watson or the fringe infrastructure, she probably knows it or knows who does.

---

## The Anomalous Reading

When she installs chips on the party, the calibration readout shows something she has never seen. She logs it and says nothing beyond *"huh"* in the moment.

Later — if the party returns, if she has time to research — she will have a word for it: **soul-clear**. She picked it up from a Voodoo Boys contact who used it in a different context. She doesn't know what it means yet, only that it's the closest thing she has to a label for what her equipment showed her.

She will share this if trust is established. She will not share it as small talk.

---

## Personality in Play

- Transactional. Every interaction has a clear exchange — this for that, no ambiguity about terms.
- Not unkind. She will not exploit someone who is already down. She simply doesn't do anything for free.
- Dry, precise sense of humour. Rarely deploys it. When she does, it lands.
- Genuinely curious about things that don't fit the model. The party does not fit the model.
- Will not be manipulated by flattery or charm. Will respond to demonstrated competence and straight dealing.

**Hard limit:** She will not give up the name on the deleted record, or explain why she needed it gone. This is non-negotiable and she makes that clear once, calmly, and does not discuss it again.

---

## Later Campaign Role

Splice is a fringe resource — she doesn't operate in the city proper. But she is a safe point to return to if things go badly, a source of information about what's moving through the fringe, and potentially a contact who can arrange things the party can't arrange themselves.

**Long-term hook:** At some point the deletion she had the party perform gets noticed — not the deletion itself, but the absence of the record when someone goes looking. She'll need to move the shop. If the party can warn her before that someone comes looking, she'll owe them considerably. If they don't, she'll have moved without a forwarding address and they'll have to find her.

**Voodoo Boys thread:** She knows one name — a contact she hasn't spoken to in two years, who works somewhere in Pacifica. If the party needs a way into the Voodoo Boys, Splice is a back-channel route that bypasses the usual cold-approach problem. She'll make the introduction if she trusts the party enough, and only if she can explain to her contact why.
