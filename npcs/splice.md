# NPC — SPLICE

**Role & Location:** Ghost ID provider and fringe fixer operating from a body shop under a Watson bridge stanchion.

**Key Characteristics:**
- Mid-thirties with optical enhancements and subdermal ports
- Distinctive circuit-trace tattoos (deliberately designed, not decorative)
- Competent, orderly, and intellectually curious about anomalies
- Transactional approach to all dealings; not unkind but doesn't work for free

**Background:** Eight years ago, she underwent a complete identity change and had a record deleted from the Watson civil database. She's deliberately vague about her past and won't discuss the deletion unless trust is significantly established.

**Services Offered:**
- Fabricated identities for automated scans and standard checkpoints
- Professional chip installation (20 minutes per person)
- Database node information and targeting assistance
- Fringe infrastructure intelligence

**Notable Discovery:** When installing chips, Splice's calibration equipment registers something unprecedented she labels "soul-clear"—terminology borrowed from Voodoo Boys contacts. She investigates this privately and shares findings only with trusted parties.

**Personality:** Dry humor, precise communication, resistant to manipulation. She respects demonstrated competence and straight dealing.

**Campaign Potential:** Serves as a safe return point, information source, and potential Voodoo Boys contact. Long-term hook involves protecting her when her deletion surfaces.

---

## The Tattoo — Act 1 Addition

The circuit-trace tattoos are **abjuration wards**. Splice doesn't know this — she had them done at a parlor in Watson and was told they were a "signal ward," something that would reduce interference with her cyberware. They look like tech. They are not.

**What they actually do:** shield her from the passive soul-drain running through Night City's population. Her calibration equipment reads her the same way it reads the party — soul-clear. She has assumed this is a hardware artefact and has not connected it to the ink.

**The Hook — The Tattoo Parlor:** whoever the artist is, they understood abjuration well enough to encode it in ink that holds for at least eight years in a magic-dead city. They may know considerably more about the Soulkiller network than anyone else the party can reach. Splice can point them there — she remembers the shop clearly.

> *GM note: the parlor artist is a lead, not an exposition source. They know something is wrong with the city's soul. They don't have the full picture. But they've been watching.*
