*Camp medic. Ripper doc (limited stock). Cyberware consultant.*

## First Impression

He comes to the party. Not subtle about it — he's been watching from the medical area since they arrived. When he introduces himself as the camp medic and offers a scan *"just precautionary, after the Quiet Mile incident,"* the curiosity underneath it is audible.

Late twenties, intense. His cyberware is clearly self-sourced and self-installed — the left optical sits a degree off from the standard mount, the subdermal ports on his forearm are spaced to his own specification rather than any factory template. He is either very confident or very reckless. Spending thirty seconds with him suggests both.

---

## Background

Self-taught. Not entirely — he had two years of formal medical training at a Night City clinic before he had to leave (he will say *"it didn't work out"* and not elaborate; Rook knows the fuller story if the party earns it). After that: books, practice, a lot of people willing to let him try things because they had no better option.

He has been the camp's medic for four years. In that time: zero lost to infection on his watch, two surgical procedures he had no business attempting that both worked, one cyberware installation that went wrong enough that he spent six months redesigning his approach before he'd touch another patient.

He salvages cyberware from wreck sites and decommissioned equipment. He knows what he's handling. He installs clean. He is realistic about what he can and can't do without city supply lines.

---

## The Scan — What He Sees

If any party member allows a medical scan (and he will ask — once directly, then twice more with diminishing subtlety):

His equipment is calibrated to Night City baseline human physiology, which includes trace ambient corruption from the soul-drain network. The party reads as **anomalously clean** — not just healthy but calibrated to a different baseline entirely. Their neural interface compatibility is off the charts in a way that makes no sense given the equipment he's scanning with.

What he says:
> *"Your cellular cohesion is — I don't have a word for this. You're not just healthy. Your baseline is different. Like you were built to a spec I've never seen. I've scanned a lot of people. You don't read like people."*

He is not alarmed. He is **riveted**. He will ask to run more tests. He will keep asking.

**What he doesn't know:** The "different baseline" is the absence of soul-drain corruption, which is present as background noise in virtually every human in Night City. He has no framework to recognise its absence as a data point. He just knows something is unusual.

**What the GM tracks:** Mote is the party's first indirect confirmation, through technical means rather than magic, that their clean-soul status is detectable and significant. His curiosity about it is harmless now. Later, if the wrong people find out what he found in those scans, it won't be.

## What He Offers

### Medical Care
Competent, practical, better-equipped than the setting implies because he has scavenged aggressively. Can treat: wounds, infections, combat injuries, basic pharmaceutical intervention, stabilisation of serious trauma.

### Cyberware Consultation
He can explain how implants work to someone who has never encountered them — clearly, without condescension, with genuine enthusiasm. He enjoys this. He finds the question *"how does it work"* deeply satisfying to answer.

**Available at camp (limited stock):**
- Trauma mods — pain damping, basic reflex augmentation. Functional. Not premium.
- Basic neural interface components — necessary for running most cyberware, minimal capability on their own
- Refurbished optical — one unit, better than nothing, not Arasaka quality
- Subdermal plating — small sections, patchwork armour. Effective for the weight.

**Not available without Night City supply lines:**
- Anything in the premium Arasaka catalogue
- Sandivistan (reflex augmentation at the high end)
- Military-grade neural links
- Anything requiring a corporate maintenance contract

He frames this honestly:
> *"I can do a lot out here. I can do more in the city. The problem with the city is that the good stuff comes from Arasaka, and Arasaka wants to know everything about what they put into you."*

He says that last part like it's a practical concern. He has no idea how right he is.

### The Warning He Gives Without Knowing Why
If the party asks about Arasaka implants specifically, Mote pauses:

> *"I've pulled their gear before. Clients come out here wanting decommissioned, off-the-grid. I've taken out Arasaka chrome that should be straightforward, and there's always an extra layer I can't account for. Not in the spec sheet. Not in any documentation I can find. I assumed it was proprietary integration code, DRM against third-party maintenance. Maybe it is."*

He goes quiet.

> *"It doesn't behave like DRM."*

## Personality in Play

- Talks faster when interested. Almost always interested in something.
- Not naive — he's lived in the Badlands four years. But he leads with curiosity before caution.
- Terrible at pretending he doesn't want to know something. His face gives him away.
- Genuinely cares about his patients. This is not common enough in Night City that it goes unnoticed.
- Will accept payment in information — particularly anything about biology, cyberware, or things that don't fit the standard model. He has a private log of medical anomalies he's encountered. The party will be several entries.

**If the party tells him the truth about where they're from:**
He goes very quiet for about ten seconds. Then:

> *"Okay. I need you to let me run every test I have. Please."*

---

## The Scan Log

Mote keeps detailed records. He'll note the party's anomalous readings in his log with his best guess at cause — "pre-corporate baseline? unknown exposure source? equipment error?" — and date it. This log exists. If someone searches his medical area later, it exists. If Arasaka ever gets access to the camp's data, it exists.

**GM note:** This is a slow-burn risk, not an immediate threat. But the record is there.

---

## Later Campaign Role

Mote is a resource and an early-warning system. He will notice changes in the party as the campaign progresses — if any of them pick up Arasaka cyberware, he'll eventually want to check the installation. He'll find the extra layer. He won't know what it is but he'll flag it.

If the party comes back damaged after Night City operations, Mote handles their recovery. He asks questions. He remembers the answers.

**Long-term hook:** At some point, Mote will ask the party to help him source something from Night City that he can't get through normal supply lines — a medical component for a camp member who needs it. The job will take them somewhere inconvenient. He'll owe them after.
