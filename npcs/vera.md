# NPC — VERA
*Nomad weapons specialist. Camp's armourer. Combat trainer.*

---

## First Impression

She finds the party before they find her. She's been watching since they arrived.

Compact, deliberate, still in the way of someone who has made sure for decades that nothing they do is wasted. The prosthetic arm is obvious — not sleek Arasaka chrome but a heavy working replacement, maintained with the same care she gives all her equipment. She moves like she's done a threat assessment on every room she's ever entered.

She doesn't introduce herself immediately. She finishes what she's doing first.

---

## Background

Ex-mercenary. Not corp — she worked for a series of smaller outfits across the Badlands and the fringe, the kind of operations that don't have marketing budgets. She was very good at her job. She settled with Rook's clan twelve years ago after a job went sideways in a way she won't describe in detail. She owes Rook a debt she's paid off three times over, but she stays because the clan keeps her people alive.

She has trained most of the camp's adults in basic weapons handling. She would not describe this as teaching — she would describe it as *maintenance*, the same way you maintain a vehicle. A person who doesn't know how to defend themselves is a liability to everyone around them.

---

## What She Offers

### Gear Assessment
She will evaluate what the party carries. Wildemount weapons read as: *unusual alloy, exceptional edge retention, balance calibrated to a spec she doesn't recognise.* Enchantments register as material anomalies — she can't name what she's seeing but she can see it.

She won't panic. She'll want to know where they were sourced.

### Trade
The camp stockpile: firearms, blades, ammunition, maintenance equipment. She trades in **utility value**, not currency. She wants:
- Working weapons in good condition
- Ammunition in calibres she can use
- Anything that increases the camp's defensive capability

She is not interested in gold. If the party tries to pay in Wildemount coinage she'll turn it over in her hands, confirm it's real metal, and offer a rate based on melt value. Fair, not generous.

### Combat Training
Available if asked, charged at cost: **labour around camp** or a **named favour** redeemable later. Not free. Nothing is free out here.

She teaches practical combat — **how to not die** rather than any particular style. Against human opponents. Against corp agents in light armour. Against the kind of threats the Badlands actually produces.

If a player character already has significant combat training, she will test that before teaching anything. She won't waste time on basics with someone who doesn't need them.

**Training benefit (mechanical suggestion):** One session = Advantage on a specific combat-relevant check of Vera's choosing, usable once before it needs to be refreshed with another session.

---

## Her Read on the Party

She can tell they're wrong in some way she can't identify. She's seen shock, injury, dissociation. This reads as none of those. They're too *present*. Alert in a way that has nothing to do with adrenaline. It sits wrong.

If she sees magic used directly:

> *"Do that again."*

Not a threat. An instruction.

**What she's actually thinking:** She was at the Quiet Mile for the extraction attempt. She saw the purple-black discharge from the tomb at a distance — not close enough to feel it, close enough to see the light was wrong. Whatever the party does looks like the **inverse** of that. She doesn't have a framework for what she saw, but she knows these two things are different in a way that matters. She wants to understand the difference because understanding things keeps people alive.

---

## Personality in Play

- Direct. Wastes no words. Does not soften assessments.
- Will say *"that technique will get you killed"* in the same tone as *"pass the salt."*
- Respects competence over almost everything else. Incompetence she will tolerate exactly once.
- Has a dry humour that comes out rarely and lands hard when it does.
- Does not respond to flattery. Does respond to someone who clearly respects their own equipment.

**If the party earns her respect:** She gives them a pre-loaded magazine from a locked case she keeps under her bunk. Doesn't explain what's in it. Just says: *"Front pocket. Not until you need it."*

---

## Later Campaign Role

Vera is a Badlands contact, not a city one. She won't follow the party into Night City.

If the party returns to the camp — for resupply, for refuge, or when things go bad in the city — Vera is there. She will have heard things. She has a network of people she trusts across the Badlands fringe who owe her information. She's not a fixer but she fills an adjacent role.

**Long-term hook:** Vera's the one who eventually tells the party that Rook's been gone three days and didn't leave a route. When Rook goes missing (if the GM wants that thread), Vera is the one who asks the party to find her. Without drama. Just: *"She doesn't disappear. Go find her."*
