## Who She Is

Rogue has been in Night City's underworld longer than most people
have been alive. She came up as a mercenary, ran with Silverhand
in the years before the raid, was one of the best operatives in
the city. That era ended at Arasaka Tower.

She survived. Silverhand didn't, not in any conventional sense.
She built something from what was left: the Afterlife — the most
legendary fixer hub in Night City. If you want serious work done,
you go to Rogue. If she doesn't know someone, they don't exist.

She is not sentimental about the past. She made her peace with
what happened at the tower. She runs a business. She maintains
neutrality between factions because neutrality is worth more
than loyalty in her line of work.

But Arasaka is the exception. She will not work for them.
She will not deal with them. And if the right people ask the
right questions, she will help take them apart.

---

## Her Role in the Campaign

### Acts 1–2: Gatekeeper and Resource
The party will need Rogue before they need Silverhand — or she's
how they find him. She is the door to Night City's serious tier.
She doesn't hand out favors. She deals in leverage, reputation,
and mutual benefit.

She will recognize the party as unusual immediately.
She has seen enough of Night City to know when something doesn't
fit, and the party doesn't fit at all. Whether that makes them
useful or dangerous depends on what they do next.

She knows Silverhand. History, not current status. When Silverhand
comes back into the picture, she and he will have things to work
out in front of the party.

### The Raid: She Plans It, She Leads the Ground Op
The assault on Arasaka Tower is her operation as much as
Silverhand's. She's the one with the contacts, the helicopter,
the crew. Silverhand is the face and the reason; Rogue is the
mechanism.

She has been building toward this for years without admitting
it to herself.

**She survives the raid.** This is important. Whatever happens
to the party and to Silverhand in that tower, Rogue gets out.
What she does with that survival — with knowing what happened —
is an Act 3 thread.

## What She Knows

- Night City's factions, power structures, and leverage points
  better than anyone
- That Arasaka does something to people's minds — she's seen
  it in former colleagues who took their contracts
- That the 2023 raid was not the end of anything
- That Silverhand was right about Arasaka, even if he was wrong
  about everything else

**She does not know:**
- That the Director is a lich
- That soul-drain is a literal mechanism, not a metaphor
- What the Soulkiller actually does (she thinks it's a
  consciousness-destruction weapon, which is partially correct)

---

## Personality

Rogue does not waste words. She makes assessments, she names
prices, she closes deals. She has been burned enough times that
her warmth is buried under several layers of professional remove.

It is still there. The party can find it, if they earn it.
Silverhand could always find it. That's part of why their history
is complicated.

She respects competence. She respects honesty. She does not
respect bravado, idealism, or people who don't understand what
they're walking into. The party will need to demonstrate they
understand the situation before she gives them anything real.
