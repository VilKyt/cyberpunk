# NPC — JOHNNY SILVERHAND

---

## Status
Dead. For fifty-four years.

Anti-corporate terrorist, musician, legend — depending on who you ask.
His engram is currently running inside V's head on a Relic chip that is
slowly overwriting V's neural patterns with his own.

The campaign is set in 2077. The raid on Arasaka Tower happened in 2023.
Silverhand died there. Smasher killed him. Arasaka ran the extraction —
the first Soulkiller that actually held. His chip is what they kept.
Then it got out. Someone made sure of that. Now V has it.

---

## Why He Matters to the Campaign

### The Soulkiller Connection
Silverhand's engram is the Director's proof-of-concept — the first clean
extraction, the blueprint the Director has been refining for fifty-four years
toward continental-scale deployment. Arasaka wants the chip back.
The party needs what's on it.

He is the only entity in Night City who:
- Spent decades watching the Director's infrastructure from the outside
- Understands Arasaka Tower's layout, security, and weak points
- Has a personal score that makes him functionally fearless about the raid
- Exists in a state — neither alive nor dead — the Director didn't fully
  account for when he designed the extraction

### How the Party Finds Him
They won't find Silverhand directly. They find his legend first — a dead man
who went to war with Arasaka and nearly won. Then the people who knew him:
Rogue. The underground. Records that shouldn't still exist.

That trail eventually leads to V. And V is carrying the ghost.

> man they're looking for is currently alive inside someone else's skull
> should land as a discovery, not a briefing.*

## The Engram

Silverhand running inside V's head is not a recording. It is not a
simulation. Whether it constitutes a real person is something Silverhand
himself would have opinions about, and loudly.

He has access to V's senses. He speaks to V. He occasionally takes
control — V has been managing this with varying success. He knows
things about Arasaka that died with his body in 2023 and survived only
because his mind did.

His reaction to the party's abilities when they eventually make contact:
contempt first. Then the specific kind of respect Silverhand has for things
that don't make sense and work anyway. He will connect the dots about the
Director faster than anyone expects — he's been watching Arasaka for decades,
he just lacked the framework. The party gives him one.

---

## What He Knows

- Arasaka Tower's architecture as of 2023. Much has changed. Not all.
- Adam Smasher — personally. That knowledge is visceral.
- That something in the tower's infrastructure predates Arasaka.
  He felt it during the extraction. He has no words for what it is.
  The party gives him words.
- Where the soft points in the network were. The Archivist can confirm
  what still applies.

---

## Playing Silverhand Through V

When the party reaches V and earns enough trust to access Silverhand
directly, he runs as an NPC voice — a second register in conversations.
He interrupts. He has opinions. He does not moderate them.

He is not the leader. He is the accelerant. He pushes situations toward
confrontation. The party decides whether to follow or brake.

His arc runs parallel to V's: both running out of time, both trying to
finish something before the clock does. The party gives him a reason to
care about the larger fight, not just the personal score.

---

## The Raid

The party's assault on Arasaka Tower runs alongside or in concert with
V's version of the same plan. Two angles, one tower.
Adam Smasher is still in there.
The Director's network still runs on Silverhand's extraction as its blueprint.
