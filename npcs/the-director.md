## Concept

The Director does not run Arasaka. He *is* Arasaka, in the same way a lich is its phylactery — the corporation is an extension of his will, his infrastructure, his body in this world. Saburo Arasaka, in this telling, either was the Director's vessel, learned from him directly, or is a constructed identity the Director has inhabited at various points over the centuries. This is deliberately left ambiguous until the party is ready to know.

He has had a thousand years to refine what he learned in Wildemount. The phylactery concept — externalising the soul to achieve immortality — has become the **Soulkiller programme**. Secure Your Soul is the retail face. The reality is a continental network that passively harvests soul-integrity data from every person running Arasaka-adjacent cyberware, which at this point is most of Night City.

He does not think of this as evil. He thinks of it as infrastructure.

---

## The Soulkiller — GM Detail

The Soulkiller is not a single weapon. It is a **distributed system**:

- **Layer 1 (Public):** Secure Your Soul — sell the public on digital immortality. Upload your engram. Live forever. Arasaka keeps the backups.
- **Layer 2 (Corporate):** The engrams aren't stored. They're processed. Soul-integrity data is extracted and fed into the phylactery network. The "backup" is a hollow copy.
- **Layer 3 (True):** The harvested soul-integrity sustains the Director's existence and powers his continental influence. The corruption seen in Arasaka agents is a byproduct — trace amounts of phylactery energy leaching into branded personnel.
- **Layer 4 (Unknown to everyone):** The network is close to a threshold. He is building toward something. The party has arrived at exactly the wrong — or right — moment.

## His View of the Party

They are an irritant. Unprocessed souls of this clarity are genuinely rare — the soul-drain has touched almost every living person on the continent in trace amounts. The party, sealed in his old lab, missed a thousand years of ambient exposure. They are **blank**. This makes them:

- A threat (unprocessed souls can resist his influence, and magic users among them could theoretically target the phylactery network)
- A resource (their soul-integrity, harvested cleanly, would be extraordinarily valuable to the network)
- A nuisance (they escaped a facility he forgot existed)

His response is proportionate to the irritant level — he sends agents, not armies. He will recalibrate if they prove to be more than he expects.

---

## The Corruption

The purple-black iridescence the party recognises from Wildemount is phylactery energy. In Wildemount it was raw and volatile — it drove people mad because it was uncontained. Over a thousand years the Director has refined it into something controlled and useful. Arasaka agents carry trace amounts. The undercity of Night City has higher concentrations — people down there who've never had proper cyberware have been absorbing ambient phylactery energy for generations. Some of them are... different. Not mad. Not undead. Something in between.

**The undercity corruption thread:** The people down there who act strange, who have a look in their eyes, who sometimes say things they shouldn't know — they're not infected in the traditional sense. They're *partially drained*. Walking around with less soul than they started with, and the remainder being slowly replaced by his network's signal. This is a horror the party can discover gradually.

## Appearance / Presence

When the Director is physically present — which is rare — he appears as an older man, immaculately presented, who is slightly wrong in a way that's hard to name. He doesn't blink at the right intervals. When he's still, he's *completely* still. His voice carries a resonance that magic-sensitive characters will feel in their chest.

In Arasaka's network he is everywhere and nowhere — his presence in the system is vast, ancient, and has been there so long that most of the corp's AI treats him as infrastructure rather than user.

---

## What He Wants

In order of priority:

1. **Completion of the Soulkiller threshold** — whatever he is building toward
2. **Collection or destruction of the party** — unprocessed souls cannot be allowed to act freely
3. **Containment of magical knowledge** — the party's existence proves magic still functions; this cannot become common knowledge
4. **Continued stability of Arasaka** — the corporation is his body; he maintains it accordingly
