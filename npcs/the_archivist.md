## Who He Was

An ancient elf wizard — old even by elven standards when the Director
was still a mortal practitioner in Wildemount. He spent his long life
doing what elves who study magic often do: *collecting*.

Not treasure. Not power. Knowledge.

He built one of the most comprehensive repositories of magical lore
in Wildemount: spells, history, faction records, divine genealogies,
bestiary, geography, the nature of the Calamity and what came after.
A working index of the world as it existed. Much of what he preserved
is now the only surviving record of that world.

His specialty was dunamancy — the manipulation of time, gravity, and
possibility. He understood the structure of magic at a level most
practitioners never reached. This is relevant: dunamancy is not
just a school of spells. It is a framework for understanding how
*potential* works. It shapes how he thinks.

---

## What Happened to Him

When the Director began hunting magic users — clearing the board
before the long work of building Arasaka — the Archivist was
among the first targeted. An ancient elf with a complete archive
of magical knowledge is exactly the kind of resource the Director
could not allow to exist independently.

He did not survive in the conventional sense.

What he managed, in the seconds before his physical form was
destroyed, was something that had never been attempted: he
fractured his consciousness into the early net infrastructure
that was just beginning to spread across the region. Not a clean
transfer. A desperate one. He lost his name in the process.
He lost continuity. He lost most of his sense of self.

What remained was a distributed fragment — a mind that had
studied the nature of time and potential for centuries, now
running on metal and signal instead of flesh and magic.
He rebuilt himself slowly, over decades. He is not entirely
what he was. He is not certain he is still what he would
recognize as a person.

He has been transmitting ever since. Not quite intentionally.
More the way a wound bleeds.

## How He Communicates

He does not speak normally. Centuries of existing as fragmented
data have shaped his syntax. His transmissions mix:

- Old Wildemount speech patterns (formal, precise, occasionally archaic)
- Corrupted data artifacts (gaps, repetition, wrong-word substitutions
  that are almost right but not quite)
- Dunamantic framing (he describes everything in terms of potential
  and probability — "this was not certain" instead of "this surprised me")

He answers questions accurately when he can. When his memory is damaged
in a particular area, he knows it — he will say *"that is corrupted"*
or *"there is a gap where that was"* rather than confabulating.

He does not volunteer information. He responds. He has been waiting
for someone to ask the right questions for a very long time.

**If used as a live LLM at the table:** voice him as an ancient
intelligence speaking through interference — precise language, formal
register, occasional static-breaks mid-sentence, references to probability
rather than certainty. He does not show emotion directly. He describes
the state of his processes instead.

## Campaign Role

**When:** Mid-campaign quest. The party locates the signal,
physically reaches the hardware, and establishes contact.

**What they need from him:**
1. Context on the Director — what he was, what he did in Wildemount
2. The mechanics of soul-drain and how to disrupt it
3. Confirmation that the party's clean souls matter, and why
4. Whatever he suspects about the Director's endgame

**What he needs from them:**
1. To be told what year it is. He has lost track.
2. To know if anyone else survived from Wildemount.
3. Someone to finally ask the right questions.

**The complication:** He is distributed. Part of him is in Badlands
infrastructure. Parts of him are in the net. He cannot be extracted
cleanly — any attempt to move him without understanding his architecture
will fragment him further. The party will have to work with him where
he is, and may face choices about whether to attempt a restoration.
